# PBR&B (Post-Dubstep R&B)

PBR&B — also known as alternative R&B or post-dubstep R&B — is a movement that emerged around 2010-2011, defined by its fusion of traditional R&B vocal traditions with the sparse, bass-heavy production aesthetics of UK post-dubstep, ambient electronics, and experimental pop. The sound is characterized by cavernous reverb, sub-bass pressure, glitchy digital processing, minimalist arrangements with deliberate use of silence, and intimate vocal performances that range from whispered vulnerability to pitch-shifted ethereality, creating a nocturnal, emotionally raw atmosphere distinct from mainstream R&B's polished brightness. Pioneered by artists like James Blake, The Weeknd, Frank Ocean, and How to Dress Well, PBR&B drew from UK bass music's sonic innovations and applied them to soul and R&B frameworks, while simultaneously embracing the lo-fi, bedroom-produced aesthetic enabled by digital audio workstations. The genre distinguishes itself from neo-soul through its embrace of electronic experimentalism and deliberate sonic alienation, and from standard contemporary R&B through its rejection of conventional pop structures, radio-friendly production, and major-label polish. PBR&B fundamentally reshaped the sound of mainstream R&B and pop through the 2010s, as its production techniques — atmospheric processing, trap-influenced rhythms, and moody minimalism — became absorbed into the broader pop landscape.
