| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Metro Area | Metro Area | 2002 | This self-titled masterpiece blended minimal techno precision with warm, organic disco strings and synth-pop sensibilities, effectively blueprinting the modern Nu-Disco sound. It prioritizes groove and atmosphere over "retro" kitsch, with other essential works found on their various Metro Area EPs. |
| Lindstrøm | Where You Go I Go Too | 2008 | A cornerstone of the "Space Disco" movement, this album features long, arpeggiated synth odysseys that merge cosmic electronic textures with a steady, driving disco pulse. Lindstrøm also defined the genre through his collaborative work It's a Feedelity Affair. |
| Todd Terje | It's Album Time | 2014 | Terje brought a playful, cinematic, and highly melodic approach to the genre, utilizing vintage synthesizers and lush percussion to bridge the gap between dancefloor and lounge. Other essential tracks can be found on his Remistakes compilation. |
| Hercules & Love Affair | Hercules & Love Affair | 2008 | This album revitalized the classic Chicago house and NYC disco connection, featuring live instrumentation and soulful vocals that lean into the genre's queer roots. The project's later work, like Blue Songs, continues this exploration of analog dance music. |
| Prins Thomas | Prins Thomas | 2010 | As a pioneer of the "Oslo Disco" scene, Prins Thomas utilizes live basslines and psychedelic percussion to create a "full-band" feel within an electronic framework. His Prins Thomas II is another essential deep dive into this organic-electronic hybrid. |
| Tiger & Woods | Through the Garden | 2011 | This duo perfected the "edit" style of Nu-Disco, taking obscure funk loops and expanding them into hypnotic, bass-heavy club tracks with surgical precision. Their follow-up, On the Green Again, remains a staple of the sample-heavy disco house subgenre. |
| Breakbot | By Your Side | 2012 | Representing the slick, "French Touch" side of Nu-Disco, this album is defined by its bright brass stabs, funky slap bass, and 80s pop production values. The artist's later album, Still Waters, further refines this polished, high-fidelity disco sound. |
| Poolside | Pacific Standard Time | 2012 | This album pioneered the "Daytime Disco" aesthetic, characterized by slowed-down tempos, breezy synthesizers, and a relaxed, sun-drenched atmosphere. Their sophomore effort, Heat, maintains this signature laid-back, yacht-rock-influenced groove. |
| Tensnake | Glow | 2014 | Moving from underground club hits to a full-length format, Tensnake's work features shimmering synths and powerful, pop-oriented vocals that define the more accessible side of the genre. His earlier Coma Cat EP is also foundational for the Nu-Disco revival. |
| The Juan MacLean | In a Dream | 2014 | A perfect fusion of DFA-style post-punk energy and classic disco arrangements, this album uses modular synths and male/female vocal interplay to create a driving, robotic funk. The Less You Know, The Better is another key entry in his discography. |
| Escort | Escort | 2011 | As a full live orchestra, Escort captures the "pure" disco sound with modern production, featuring soaring strings, heavy horns, and a relentless rhythm section. Their follow-up, Animal Nature, continues this tradition of high-concept, big-band disco. |
| Aeroplane | In Flight Entertainment | 2011 | This compilation/mix album showcases the artist's ability to blend melancholy piano melodies with Balearic disco beats and psychedelic synth-pop. While largely known for remixes, his original We Can't Fly album is equally essential for its cinematic scope. |
| Holy Ghost! | Holy Ghost! | 2011 | This album utilizes a massive array of vintage analog gear to create a sound that is both nostalgic and modern, focusing on complex percussion and catchy synth hooks. Their later work, Dynamics, leans further into the synth-pop side of the disco spectrum. |
| L'Impératrice | Matahari | 2018 | This French outfit brings a sophisticated, chic sensibility to the genre, combining spacey synthesizers with virtuosic bass playing and dreamy, ethereal vocals. Their debut Odyssée EP and the album Tako Tsubo are also highly influential in the modern scene. |
| Crazy P | When We On | 2011 | Combining live instrumentation with a deep house pulse, this album represents the "soulful" side of Nu-Disco, characterized by sultry vocals and warm, organic grooves. Their album Age of the Ego is another definitive example of their refined, mature sound. |
| Horse Meat Disco | Love and Dancing | 2020 | As the torchbearers of the modern disco club scene, this collective creates tracks that celebrate the genre's history through authentic arrangements and diverse vocalists. Their various Horse Meat Disco compilations are also essential for understanding the genre's DNA. |
| Moon Boots | First Landing | 2017 | This album blends R&B-inflected vocals with shimmering Nu-Disco production, focusing on lush pads and "feel-good" melodies. His follow-up, Bimini Road, further explores the intersection of deep house and polished disco. |
| Midnight Star | Planetary Invasion | 1984 | While technically "Electro-Funk," this album's heavy use of vocoders and synth-bass served as a primary inspiration for the Nu-Disco movement's aesthetic. It bridges the gap between the original disco era and the electronic revival that followed. |
| Kasper Bjørke | Standing on Top of Utopia | 2010 | This Danish producer offers a darker, more melancholic take on the genre, blending disco rhythms with post-punk textures and indie-pop vocals. His album Fool continues this exploration of "sad disco" aesthetics. |
| Gushh | In the Midnight | 2015 | A standout for its use of vibrant, "neon" synth lines and heavy, compressed drum machines that mirror the Retrowave movement but keep a strict disco-funk structure. The artist's various singles provide a consistent look into the digital side of Nu-Disco. |
| Faze Action | Moving Cities | 1999 | Early pioneers who helped bridge the gap between Acid Jazz and Nu-Disco by incorporating live cello, flute, and percussion into electronic grooves. Their album Stratus Energy is another essential example of their organic-electronic fusion. |
| Classixx | Hanging Gardens | 2013 | This album is a masterclass in clean, melodic production, featuring bright synth textures and a breezy, Los Angeles-inspired atmosphere. Their follow-up, Faraway Reach, expands on this with more diverse vocal collaborations. |
| Roosevelt | Roosevelt | 2016 | Melding indie-rock sensibilities with a steady 4/4 disco beat, this album defines the "Indie-Dance" side of Nu-Disco with its hazy guitars and infectious synth hooks. His subsequent album, Polydans, leans even more heavily into pure dancefloor grooves. |
| Yuksek | Nosso Ritmo | 2020 | Representing the high-energy, rhythmic side of the French scene, this album incorporates Latin percussion and funky guitar riffs into a modern electronic framework. His earlier work Living on the Edge of Time is also a key Nu-Disco/Electropop hybrid. |
| Satin Jackets | Panorama Pacifico | 2016 | This project defines the "Dreamy" or "Balearic" Nu-Disco sound, using soft-focus synthesizers and smooth, soulful vocals to create a sense of escapism. The album Solar Nights continues this signature high-gloss, atmospheric production. |
| Tuxedo | Tuxedo | 2015 | This collaboration between Mayer Hawthorne and Jake One is a dedicated tribute to 80s boogie and funk, featuring crisp production and infectious pop-soul vocals. Their later albums, Tuxedo II and III, maintain this strict devotion to the "G-Funk" disco aesthetic. |
| Luxxury | It's Not a Movie | 2019 | Known for his "One Million Strong" edits, Luxxury's original work features shimmering, West Coast-inspired disco with a focus on bass guitar and light-as-air synth pads. His various Deep & Disco compilations are also highly representative of the genre. |
| Miami Horror | Illumination | 2010 | This album blends 80s synth-pop nostalgia with modern dance production, utilizing soaring choruses and intricate electronic layering. Their follow-up, All Possible Futures, further explores the psychedelic side of the Nu-Disco spectrum. |
| Late Night Tuff Guy | LNTG: The Album | 2017 | A master of the "re-edit," this artist defines the genre by stripping classic tracks down to their most hypnotic, bass-heavy elements and rebuilding them for modern sound systems. His various Tuff Cuts releases are essential for any Nu-Disco DJ. |
