# Blue-Eyed Soul

Blue-eyed soul is a term used to describe R&B and soul music performed by white artists who authentically embrace the vocal stylings, emotional intensity, and musical traditions of Black American soul music. Originating in the early 1960s with British Invasion acts and American groups influenced by Motown, Stax, and Atlantic Records, the genre is characterized by powerful, gospel-inflected vocals delivered over classic soul arrangements featuring horns, Hammond organs, and tight rhythm sections. The production often draws from Philadelphia soul, Southern soul, and funk traditions, with artists ranging from gritty, shouting performers to smooth, sophisticated crooners. Blue-eyed soul distinguishes itself through the sincere adoption of R&B conventions rather than mere imitation, with many artists recording at legendary studios like Muscle Shoals and American Sound. The genre has evolved across decades, from the raw 1960s British and American acts through the polished yacht-soul of the late 1970s to modern revivalists who meticulously recreate vintage Motown and soul aesthetics.
