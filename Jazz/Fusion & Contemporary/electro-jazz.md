# Electro Jazz

Electro jazz is a genre that merges jazz improvisation, harmony, and instrumentation with electronic production techniques including sampling, drum programming, synthesizers, and digital effects processing. Emerging in the early 1980s with Herbie Hancock's pioneering fusion of hip-hop and jazz keyboards, the genre evolved through the 1990s European nu-jazz movement and the 2000s LA beat scene into a diverse field encompassing downtempo lounge, breakbeat-driven cinematic compositions, and glitch-infused free jazz. The sound is characterized by the interplay between live acoustic instruments — particularly trumpet, saxophone, piano, and upright bass — and programmed electronic rhythms drawn from trip-hop, drum and bass, house, and IDM. Electro jazz distinguishes itself from jazz fusion through its reliance on studio-based electronic production rather than rock instrumentation, and from electronic music through its commitment to jazz harmonic language and improvisational freedom. The genre has developed strong regional identities from the Scandinavian nu-jazz of Norway to the French Touch lounge sound and the sample-heavy beat music of Los Angeles.
