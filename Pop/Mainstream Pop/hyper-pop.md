# Hyper Pop

Hyper pop is an internet-born genre that emerged in the mid-2010s from the PC Music label and its surrounding online community, characterized by its maximalist approach to pop music that pushes production techniques to absurd, overwhelming extremes through pitch-shifted vocals, distorted synthesizers, abrasive bass, and breakneck tempo changes that deconstruct and reassemble pop conventions. The sound draws from bubblegum pop, nightcore, dubstep, happy hardcore, hip-hop, emo, and noise music simultaneously, creating a deliberately artificial, hyperreal aesthetic where saccharine melodies collide with industrial harshness and glitchy digital artifacts. Pioneered by A. G. Cook, SOPHIE, and the PC Music collective, and later popularized by artists like 100 gecs and Charli XCX, hyper pop encompasses both the label's pristine synthetic maximalism and the more chaotic, DIY digicore scene that proliferated on platforms like SoundCloud and Discord. The genre distinguishes itself from standard electropop through its deliberate embrace of digital ugliness and excess, and from experimental electronic music through its unwavering commitment to pop song structures and hooks however warped they may become. Hyper pop has spawned an entire ecosystem of internet-native subcultures — digicore, glitchcore, and scancore — that continue to blur the boundaries between pop, punk, hip-hop, and avant-garde electronic music.
