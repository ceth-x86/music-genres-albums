# Frenchcore

Frenchcore is a high-tempo subgenre of hardcore electronic music that originated in the French free party and teknival scene during the late 1990s, defined by its characteristic "bouncy" reverse-bass kick drums and relentless tempos typically ranging from 190 to 210 BPM. The sound evolved from the harder edges of gabber and industrial hardcore, developing a distinctive rhythmic signature where the kick drum features a pronounced "galloping" or "rolling" quality created through reverse bass distortion techniques. Production encompasses a wide range from dark, industrial-tinged minimalism to euphoric, orchestral arrangements incorporating classical piano, operatic vocals, and melodic synthesizers. Frenchcore distinguishes itself from gabber through its specific kick-drum architecture and its roots in the nomadic free party culture of rural France, and from speedcore through its maintenance of musicality and danceability despite extreme tempos. The genre has evolved from underground rave tool-tracks into a globally popular festival sound, with modern producers adding psytrance triplets, symphonic elements, and video game-inspired themes while maintaining the foundational bouncing kick pattern.
