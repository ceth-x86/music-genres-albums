# Speedcore

Speedcore is an extreme subgenre of hardcore electronic music defined by its extraordinarily high tempos, typically ranging from 300 BPM to over 1000 BPM, where rhythm itself becomes a textural and tonal element rather than a purely percussive one. The sound is characterized by massively distorted kick drums that function as both rhythmic and melodic instruments, abrasive industrial noise textures, and a production aesthetic that embraces sonic brutality as its primary artistic statement, drawing from gabber, industrial, noise, and grindcore traditions. Emerging in the early-to-mid 1990s from the European hardcore techno underground, speedcore pushed gabber's already aggressive tempo and distortion to their logical extremes, spawning further subgenres like splittercore, extratone, and terrorcore that explored the boundaries between rhythm and drone. The genre distinguishes itself from gabber and industrial hardcore through its commitment to velocity as the defining parameter, often rendering traditional danceability irrelevant in favor of a visceral, overwhelming physical experience. Speedcore has developed distinct regional scenes — German terror, Japanese J-Core, Australian Bloody Fist — each bringing unique aesthetic priorities while maintaining the genre's foundational principle that speed itself is the message.
