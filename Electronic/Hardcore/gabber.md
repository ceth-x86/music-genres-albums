# Gabber

Gabber is an extreme form of electronic dance music that originated in Rotterdam, the Netherlands, in the early 1990s, defined by its punishing tempos of 160-200+ BPM and heavily distorted kick drums produced primarily on the Roland TR-909. The genre emerged as a rebellious, working-class response to the perceived elitism of Amsterdam's house scene, establishing its own raw, aggressive identity through the iconic "hoover" synth sounds, dark atmospheric stabs, and minimalist arrangements that prioritize sheer percussive impact. Gabber culture developed a distinctive identity around events like Thunderdome and labels such as Mokum and Rotterdam Records, complete with its own fashion, dance styles, and anti-establishment attitude. The sound ranges from the melodic "happy gabber" and "artcore" strains to the punishing extremes of terrorcore and industrial hardcore, but all share the signature distorted kick as their rhythmic foundation. Gabber distinguishes itself from other hardcore electronic styles through its Dutch cultural roots, its emphasis on distortion as a primary sonic element, and its evolution into the broader "mainstream hardcore" scene that continues to fill massive European festivals.
