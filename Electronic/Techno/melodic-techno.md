| Tale Of Us | Endless | 2017 | This duo defined the "Afterlife" sound, blending haunting classical piano arrangements with deep, driving techno percussion. Other essential releases include their North Star / Silent Space EP. |
| Stephan Bodzin | Powers of Ten | 2015 | Bodzin is a master of the Moog synthesizer, creating soaring, elastic lead lines that provide a heavy emotional narrative over club-ready beats. His albums Herzblut and Boavista are also genre cornerstones. |
| Maceo Plex | Solar | 2017 | This album showcases a blend of dark, futuristic textures and complex melodic layers that feel both cinematic and industrial. He is also known for Life Index. |
| Recondite | Hinterland | 2013 | Recondite specializes in a "lower-case" melodic style, using acidic but melancholic synths to create a somber, atmospheric tension. Placid and On Acid further explore this moody aesthetic. |
| Mind Against | Atlant | 2013 | Known for deep, psychedelic soundscapes, this duo uses shimmering synth pads and steady, hypnotic rhythms to induce a trance-like state. Their Solaris EP is another vital reference point. |
| Ben Bohmer | Breathing | 2019 | Bohmer leans into the brighter side of the genre, using lush orchestral strings and organic piano melodies over a steady techno pulse. Begin Again is another key work in his discography. |
| Adriatique | Nude | 2018 | This album features long-form, evolving arrangements that focus on subtle harmonic shifts and crystalline production. Their Soulweaver EP is also highly influential. |
| Patrice Baumel | Glutes | 2017 | Baumel focuses on high-energy, "big room" melodic structures characterized by sweeping filter sweeps and percussive precision. His Speicher 89 release is another essential listen. |
| Kiasmos | Kiasmos | 2014 | A collaboration between Olafur Arnalds and Janus Rasmussen, this album bridges neoclassical piano with minimal, glitchy techno rhythms. Their Swept EP is also a landmark of the style. |
| Agents Of Time | Universo | 2022 | These artists are known for their hardware-heavy approach, creating intricate, improvised-feeling lead lines and driving bass. Their Forest of Lies EP is also highly regarded. |
| Colyn | Resolve | 2019 | Colyn's sound is characterized by "spacey" synth textures and uplifting, euphoric melodies that retain a techno edge. He is also celebrated for the Oxygen Levels Low EP. |
| Mathame | Memo | 2022 | This duo utilizes cinematic vocals and dramatic, operatic synth swells to create an epic, storytelling atmosphere. Their single Skywalking remains one of the genre's biggest anthems. |
| Artbat | Upperground | 2019 | This release solidified their signature sound of heavy, rolling basslines paired with sharp, infectious melodic hooks. Their Planeta EP is another definitive example of their style. |
| Max Cooper | Emergence | 2016 | Cooper blends scientific concepts with intricate glitch-techno and sweeping, emotive synth washes. Human and Yearning for the Infinite are equally essential. |
| Jan Blomqvist | Disconnected | 2018 | Blomqvist brings a "concert" feel to techno, incorporating his own vocals and live instrumentation over deep, melodic electronic beds. Remote Control is another key album. |
| Innellea | The World Returns | 2019 | Characterized by distorted, aggressive synth leads and unconventional rhythmic patterns, this album pushes the genre's boundaries. The Five Phases project is also a must-listen. |
| Monolink | Amniotic | 2018 | Monolink bridges the gap between singer-songwriter aesthetics and melodic techno, featuring acoustic guitar and processed vocals. Under Darkening Skies follows a similar path. |
| Sebastien Leger | Lost Miracle | 2019 | Leger uses modular synthesis to create intricate, "oriental-inspired" melodies and organic percussive textures. His tracks Lanarka and Giza are genre-defining. |
| Woo York | Land of Heroes | 2018 | This duo utilizes hardware to create a gritty, analog sound that remains focused on dark, driving melody. Their Chuchotement EP is also highly influential. |
| Rodriguez Jr. | Baobab | 2017 | Known for his elegant use of vintage synths, his music features warm, liquid melodies and sophisticated rhythmic layering. Bittersweet is another essential album. |
| Oliver Koletzki | The Arc of Alchemist | 2019 | Koletzki blends organic world-music elements with crisp, melodic techno production. Made of Air and Fire in the Jungle are also defining works. |
| Dominik Eulberg | Mannigfaltig | 2019 | Eulberg uses nature-inspired sounds and complex, evolving synth arrangements to create "eco-techno." Heimische Gefilde is another masterclass in the genre. |
| Fideles | Impressum | 2017 | This duo creates a balance between club-driven energy and deep, emotional synth journeys. Their The Wave and Night After Night EPs are core genre texts. |
| Kevin de Vries | Dragonberry | 2020 | De Vries specializes in high-octane, "trance-leaning" techno that features powerful, soaring lead synths. His track Dance With Me is a modern classic. |
| Einmusik | I.D. | 2017 | This album is a journey through shimmering pads, driving bass, and intricate, interlocking melodies. Serenade and Small Talk are also significant releases. |
| Extrawelt | Fear of an Extra Planet | 2017 | Known for their "crunchy" textures, they combine distorted percussion with haunting, dark melodic sequences. Schone Neue Extrawelt is a foundational genre album. |
| Guy J | The Trees, The Sky & The Sea | 2015 | A pioneer of the more hypnotic side of melodic techno, Guy J uses long, evolving build-ups and intricate layering. Espoir and 1000 Words are also essential. |
| Nox Vahn | Earthshine | 2020 | This artist creates deep, cavernous atmospheres paired with bright, crystalline synth motifs. He is also known for the track Walking with Lions. |
| Hidden Empire | Mind Palace | 2018 | This duo focuses on a sleek, high-tech sound with punchy drums and sharp, arpeggiated synth leads. Lost In Space is another definitive release. |
| HOSH | Stories from Sa Talaia | 2017 | This album explores organic textures and groovy, melodic structures designed for open-air sunsets. His track Midnight (The Hanging Tree) is a global hit. |
| Yotto | Hyperfall | 2018 | Yotto blends deep house sensibilities with the driving energy and grand melodies of melodic techno. Erased Dreams is another key project of his. |
| GHEIST | Zukunft | 2021 | This group integrates live vocals and melancholic piano with heavy, driving techno foundations. Their Frequent Traveller EP is also highly influential. |
| Township Rebellion | Vesta | 2016 | Known for dark, booming low-ends and epic, theatrical synth progressions that build intense tension. Their Luminous release is another genre staple. |
| Ae:ther | Me | 2019 | This album features abstract, ethereal melodies and deep, dubby rhythms that feel both futuristic and introspective. His Cielo EP is also essential. |
| Hunter/Game | Adaptation | 2016 | This duo creates deep, echoing soundscapes that evoke vast, cold landscapes through subtle melodic shifts. Silence is another definitive release. |
| Joris Voorn | *\\* | 2019 | Voorn combines sophisticated house-influenced rhythms with shimmering, complex melodic patterns. Nobody Knows is another essential album in his catalog. |
| Massano | In My System | 2022 | A rising star of the "Afterlife" sound, Massano uses aggressive, industrial-tinged synths and driving, rhythmic melodies. His Solitude EP is equally definitive. |
| Oostil | Quimera | 2019 | This project focuses on a minimalist approach to melody, using sparse but emotionally heavy synth lines and deep grooves. They are also known for Singularity. |
| Tibi Dabo | Mirror Ball | 2020 | This artist creates a more playful, groovy version of the genre with bright synths and shuffling percussion. The Distance We Share is another notable release. |
| VNTM | Counteractive | 2021 | VNTM uses dark, acidic lead lines and heavy, warehouse-ready kicks to create a "dark melodic" atmosphere. Dark Echoes is another core project. |
| Christian Loffler | Mare | 2016 | Loffler blends field recordings and organic instrumentation with soft, melancholic techno beats. A Forest and Lys are also essential listening. |
| Sasha | Scene Delete | 2016 | While a veteran of progressive house, this album saw Sasha embrace the ambient and melodic techno world with beatless and deep-rhythm tracks. Refracted is also relevant. |
| Henry Saiz | Night & Day | 2013 | Saiz creates highly complex, multi-layered electronic music that weaves together vocals, synths, and organic sounds. Digital Mirages is another key work. |
| Kollektiv Turmstrasse | Rebellion der Traumer | 2010 | A foundational album that introduced a cinematic, melodic sensibility to the minimal techno movement. Unity is another significant release. |
| Gabriel Ananda | Bambusbeast | 2007 | An early pioneer, Ananda's sound is defined by whimsical, interlocking melodies and a warm, organic feel. Selected Works covers much of his essential output. |
| Johannes Brecht | Music for Space Travel | 2019 | Brecht uses his classical background to create sophisticated orchestral arrangements within a techno framework. Voix Grave is another essential EP. |
| Oliver Huntemann | Propaganda | 2017 | Huntemann delivers a darker, more mechanical version of melodic techno with sharp, metallic synth stabs. Paranoia and H-3 are also core albums. |
| Stereo Express | Monado | 2021 | This album features driving, high-tempo rhythms and big, euphoric synth hooks designed for peak-time energy. Rise of the 2nd Sun is another key work. |
| Undercatt | Abyssal | 2019 | This duo focuses on sleek, futuristic production with deep basslines and haunting, minimalist melodies. Their track Britannia is a genre classic. |
| Anna Caragnano & Donato Dozzy | Sintetizzatrice | 2015 | While experimental, this album uses the voice as a melodic synth, defining the deep, hypnotic side of the genre. Dozzy's K is also a landmark. |
