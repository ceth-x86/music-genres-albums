# Synthwave / Retrowave

Synthwave, also known as Retrowave or Outrun, is a predominantly instrumental electronic genre that emerged in the late 2000s as an affectionate homage to the synthesizer-heavy soundtracks, film scores, and pop music of the 1980s. The sound is built on lush analog-style pads, driving arpeggiated basslines, gated reverb drums, and soaring lead synths that evoke the neon-lit aesthetics of 1980s action films, arcade games, and VHS culture. Producers typically employ vintage or vintage-emulating synthesizers -- Yamaha DX7, Roland Juno-106, Moog Sub -- alongside modern DAWs to achieve an authentic retro-futuristic timbre. The genre spans a wide spectrum from the sun-drenched, melodic "dreamwave" of FM-84 and Timecop1983 to the aggressive, horror-influenced "darksynth" of Perturbator and Carpenter Brut, which incorporates distorted guitars and industrial intensity. Synthwave's visual culture is inseparable from its music, defined by neon grids, palm trees, DeLoreans, and cyberpunk cityscapes that have become iconic internet aesthetics. The movement grew almost entirely through online communities and platforms like Bandcamp and YouTube, making it one of the first major genres to develop primarily through digital culture.

| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Kavinsky | OutRun | 2013 | Anchored by the iconic "Nightcall," this album solidified the "Outrun" aesthetic of driving synth-bass, cinematic atmosphere, and 80s action movie tropes. It defines the cool, nocturnal side of the genre; other essential works include the Teddy Boy and 1986 EPs. |
| Lazerhawk | Redline | 2010 | A quintessential example of high-energy, "fast" synthwave, featuring aggressive saw-tooth leads and driving arpeggiators that mimic a high-speed car chase. He also explored darker, spacier realms in Visitors and Skull and Shark. |
| The Midnight | Endless Summer | 2016 | This album perfectly captures the "Dreamwave" nostalgia, blending lush pads, soulful vocals, and signature saxophone solos to evoke 80s coastal sunsets. Other notable releases that define their polished pop-synth sound include Days of Thunder and Kids. |
| Com Truise | Galactic Melt | 2011 | Defined by "mid-fi synth-wave," this album uses warped, squelchy analog textures and complex, "slowed-down" funk rhythms to create a unique, hazy retro-future. Iteration and In Decay further expanded this signature textured sound. |
| Perturbator | Dangerous Days | 2014 | While later moving toward Industrial, this album is a pillar of "Darksynth," utilizing heavy, distorted basslines and occult aesthetics inspired by 80s horror and cyberpunk. I Am the Night and The Uncanny Valley are also fundamental dark entries. |
| Miami Nights 1984 | Turbulence | 2012 | Often credited with pioneering the aesthetic, this album features bright, shimmering FM synthesis and drum machines that scream 1980s Miami luxury. The album Early Summer is another masterclass in the genre's sun-drenched origins. |
| Gunship | Gunship | 2015 | This self-titled debut mastered the cinematic, vocal-led synthwave style, using vintage gear to create huge, melodic soundscapes that feel like an 80s blockbuster credits sequence. Dark All Day is another essential, genre-expanding follow-up. |
| Mitch Murder | Current Events | 2011 | A purist of the genre, his work sounds indistinguishable from actual 1980s production, focusing on upbeat melodies and accurate Yamaha DX7-style FM synthesis. Interceptor and Selection 1 are equally vital for the "authentic" retro sound. |
| Carpenter Brut | Trilogy | 2015 | Collecting his three EPs, this work defines the "Slasherwave" sub-style, mixing aggressive electronic beats with heavy-metal energy and John Carpenter-esque horror motifs. Leather Teeth is another notable evolution of this high-octane sound. |
| FM-84 | Atlas | 2016 | Widely considered the "gold standard" for melodic, optimistic synthwave, it features pristine production, soaring choruses, and a heavy emphasis on "The Summer of '84" nostalgia. The single "Running in the Night" remains a genre anthem. |
| Power Glove | EP 1 | 2012 | This duo focuses on the grittier, video-game-inspired side of the genre, using heavy gated-reverb drums and menacing synth stabs reminiscent of 80s sci-fi soundtracks. Their work on the Far Cry 3: Blood Dragon OST is also foundational. |
| Timecop1983 | Night Drive | 2017 | The definitive "Dreamwave" artist, specializing in emotive, slow-tempo tracks with shimmering pads and romantic melodies that evoke a sense of longing. Journeys and Lovers are also essential for this softer, atmospheric style. |
| Trevor Something | Trevor Something Does Not Exist | 2014 | This album blends Vaporwave sensibilities with Retrowave, featuring slowed-down vocals and a thick, lo-fi atmosphere that feels like a forgotten VHS tape. Death Dream and Soulless are other key entries in his moody discography. |
| Dance with the Dead | The Outbreak | 2014 | A leading force in "Horror-synth," this album fuses driving electronic beats with prominent 80s-style metal guitar riffs to create a "zombie apocalypse" vibe. Near Dark and B-Sides: Vol. 1 are also highly influential. |
| Dynatron | Escape Velocity | 2012 | Focuses on the "Space-Synth" side of the movement, using sweeping analog textures and cosmic melodies to create a sense of interstellar exploration. Aeternus and The Rigel Solution are also standout atmospheric works. |
| Waveshaper | Station Nova | 2016 | This album is a masterclass in hardware-based production, utilizing crisp arpeggios and bright leads that sound like a 1980s tech-demonstration video. Solar Pulse and The Terminal are other essential "high-tech" retro albums. |
| Robert Parker | Crystal City | 2016 | Known for a "funkier" approach, this album uses disco-inspired basslines and bright synth brass to create an upbeat, 80s nightlife atmosphere. Endless Love and Drive also showcase his ability to blend French House with Retrowave. |
| HOME | Odyssey | 2014 | While leaning slightly toward Chillwave, this album's use of vintage synth patches and warm, nostalgic melodies made it a massive influence on the broader Retrowave community. Falling Into Place is another key work in this relaxed style. |
| Mega Drive | 198XAD | 2014 | A cornerstone of "Cyberpunk" synthwave, this album features dark, mechanical rhythms and glitchy textures that evoke a dystopian future. Hardwired and 2112 are similarly essential for fans of the "Dark-Future" aesthetic. |
| Garth Knight | Goliath | 2014 | This album emphasizes the "Cinematic" aspect, featuring slow-building tension and heavy, orchestral-synth arrangements that feel like a lost action-movie soundtrack. Auto-Magik is another notable release showing his versatile style. |
| Le Matos | Join Us | 2013 | Hailing from Montreal, this duo specializes in rhythmic, pulsating synths with a strong emphasis on melody and 80s film score structures. Their soundtrack for the film Turbo Kid is also a defining moment for the genre's visibility. |
| Scandroid | Scandroid | 2016 | Klayton's project merges Retrowave with modern production and vocals, creating a polished, sci-fi concept album filled with 80s pop hooks and guitar-synth hybrids. Monochrome and The Darkness and The Light are also core works. |
| College | Northern Council | 2008 | An early pioneer of the "minimalist" French synth sound, focusing on simple, evocative melodies and sparse arrangements that prioritize mood over complexity. The A Real Hero EP (with Electric Youth) is his most famous contribution. |
| Electric Youth | Innerworld | 2014 | Defining the "Synthpop" crossover of the genre, this album features breathy vocals and shimmering, soft-synth production that feels like an 80s teen drama. Their work on the Drive soundtrack helped launch the modern movement. |
| GosT | Behemoth | 2015 | A pivotal "Darksynth" release that leans into the "Slasher" aesthetic, using heavy distortion and fast, aggressive tempos that border on electronic dance music. Non Paradisi is another essential, darker-themed project. |
| Alex & Tokyo Rose | Akuma | 2017 | This collaboration defines the "Aggressive Darksynth" sound, blending heavy industrial-style beats with high-energy synth leads and a dark, urban aesthetic. Akuma II further refined this heavy, cinematic approach. |
| Daniel Deluxe | Corruptor | 2016 | Features a "minimalist dark" sound, focusing on heavy, rhythmic basslines and a clean but menacing production style perfect for cyberpunk settings. Instruments of Murder is another notable work in his dark, driving discography. |
| Wolf and Raven | Ace of Spades | 2015 | This album leans into the "shredwave" side of the genre, featuring heavy use of synth-guitar solos and high-energy 80s power-metal structures. Renegades is another great example of their uptempo, heroic sound. |
| September 87 | Bad Dream Baby | 2017 | A standout for its use of authentic 80s pop production techniques, focusing on soulful vocals, gated drums, and funky synth-bass. The title track is often cited as a "perfect" example of 80s-inspired songwriting. |
| Droid Bishop | Beyond the Blue | 2014 | Blends Prog-rock sensibilities with Retrowave, featuring complex synth arrangements and melodic guitar work that creates a sophisticated, "Art-Synth" vibe. Lost in Space and Music of the Spheres also highlight this technical style. |
