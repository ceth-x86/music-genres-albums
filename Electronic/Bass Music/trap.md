# Trap

Trap is a hip-hop subgenre that emerged from Atlanta's Southern rap scene in the early 2000s, named after the "trap houses" where drugs were sold, and defined by its thunderous Roland TR-808 kick drums, rapid hi-hat rolls, layered synthesizer melodies, and lyrical focus on street life, hustle, and excess. The genre's production aesthetic is built on booming 808 sub-bass, sharp snare patterns, ominous orchestral stabs, and eerie minor-key synth lines, creating a cinematic tension that ranges from menacing to triumphant. Pioneered by producers like Shawty Redd, Zaytoven, and Lex Luger and popularized by artists like T.I., Jeezy, and Gucci Mane, trap evolved through several distinct phases: the gritty street narratives of the early 2000s, the aggressive maximalism of the Lex Luger era, the melodic and psychedelic evolution driven by Future and Young Thug, and the emo-trap crossover of Juice WRLD and Lil Uzi Vert. Atlanta remains the genre's spiritual capital, though trap's influence has spread globally, reshaping mainstream pop, electronic dance music, and hip-hop production worldwide. By the mid-2010s, trap had become the dominant commercial sound in American music, with its rhythmic vocabulary -- particularly the triplet flow and rolling hi-hat patterns -- becoming the default template for contemporary hip-hop production.

| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| T.I. | Trap Muzik | 2003 | Often credited with popularizing the term "Trap," this album features the heavy brass and 808-laden production that defined the genre's early Atlanta identity. His other essential works include I'm Serious and King. |
| Young Jeezy | Let's Get It: Thug Motivation 101 | 2005 | This album established the "Snowman" persona and the cinematic, triumphant synth-heavy trap sound produced largely by Shawty Redd. The Inspiration and Trap or Die are also cornerstone releases. |
| Gucci Mane | Trap House | 2005 | A foundational text of the genre, it features Zaytoven's signature keyboard-driven production and Gucci's idiosyncratic flow. The State vs. Radric Davis and Trap Back are equally vital to the trap canon. |
| Waka Flocka Flame | Flockaveli | 2010 | Produced almost entirely by Lex Luger, this album introduced a more aggressive, orchestral, and "aggressive" sound that shifted the genre's energy toward the "mosh pit" aesthetic. Triple F Life is another high-energy highlight. |
| Rick Ross | Teflon Don | 2010 | While Ross touches on many styles, this album contains "B.M.F.," which solidified the Lex Luger-produced "maximalist" trap sound of the early 2010s. Port of Miami and Rather You Than Me are also essential listens. |
| Future | DS2 (Dirty Sprite 2) | 2015 | This album is the pinnacle of the "moody," drug-inflected, and psychedelic trap sound characterized by Metro Boomin's dark, hazy production. Future's Monster and 56 Nights are also genre-defining mixtapes. |
| Young Thug | Barter 6 | 2015 | This project showcased the avant-garde, vocal-inflected side of trap, emphasizing elastic flows and unconventional melodies over minimal, bass-heavy beats. Jeffery and So Much Fun are other pivotal releases. |
| Migos | Culture | 2017 | Featuring the triplet flow that became a genre standard, this album perfected the polished, ad-lib heavy "pop-trap" sound that dominated the late 2010s. Their mixtape Y.R.N. (Young Rich Niggas) is another essential entry. |
| Travis Scott | Rodeo | 2015 | This album merged trap's 808 foundations with grand, atmospheric production and distorted vocals, pushing the genre toward a more "psychedelic" and widescreen aesthetic. Astroworld is his other massive contribution. |
| Chief Keef | Finally Rich | 2012 | While often associated with Drill, Keef's debut significantly influenced the "melodic" yet gritty street-trap sound that moved from Chicago to the global stage. Back from the Dead is his other essential blueprint. |
| 21 Savage | Issa Album | 2017 | Known for his menacing, whisper-quiet delivery and minimalist production (often by Metro Boomin), this album defines the dark, "noir" side of the trap genre. Savage Mode and i am > i was are also top-tier works. |
| Lil Uzi Vert | Luv Is Rage 2 | 2017 | This album defines "emo-trap," blending high-energy trap rhythms with pop-punk sensibilities and melancholic, melodic vocal runs. Lil Uzi Vert vs. The World is another definitive project. |
| Gunna | Drip or Drown 2 | 2019 | Gunna perfected the "mumble" or "smooth" trap style, where the voice acts as another melodic instrument over fluid, guitar-laden beats. WUNNA and Drip Harder (with Lil Baby) are also essential. |
| Lil Baby | My Turn | 2020 | A masterclass in modern Atlanta trap, featuring relentless flows and crisp, heavy-hitting production that defines the current "commercial" trap standard. Harder Than Ever and The Voice of the Heroes are other notable titles. |
| Kodak Black | Painting Pictures | 2017 | Bringing a distinct Florida "swamp" sound to the genre, this album features raw storytelling over bouncy, melodic trap production. Project Baby 2 and Dying to Live are other essential listens. |
| Playboi Carti | Die Lit | 2018 | This album redefined the genre through minimalism, focusing on "vibe" and repetitive, rhythmic ad-libs over traditional lyricism, often called "mumble rap." His self-titled debut and Whole Lotta Red are also foundational. |
| 2 Chainz | Based on a T.R.U. Story | 2012 | This album represents the "fun" side of trap, utilizing clever wordplay and high-gloss, club-ready production to bring the genre to the masses. B.O.A.T.S. II: Me Time and Pretty Girls Like Trap Music are also key. |
| Juice WRLD | Goodbye & Good Riddance | 2018 | A cornerstone of the melodic and "emo" trap movement, focusing on heartbreak and internal struggle over melancholic, guitar-driven 808 beats. Death Race for Love is his other major genre contribution. |
| Metro Boomin | Not All Heroes Wear Capes | 2018 | As the genre's most influential modern producer, this project highlights his ability to craft cinematic, dark, and cohesive trap soundscapes for various vocalists. Heroes & Villains is another essential production showcase. |
| Roddy Ricch | Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial | 2019 | This album exemplifies the melodic "West Coast" influenced trap sound, utilizing catchy hooks and piano-heavy production. Feed Tha Streets II is his other major project. |
| ASAP Rocky | Long. Live. ASAP | 2013 | While hailing from Harlem, Rocky brought a "cloud" trap aesthetic to the genre, blending Southern Houston influences with high-fashion, atmospheric production. Live. Love. ASAP is his other essential mixtape. |
| Rae Sremmurd | SremmLife | 2015 | Produced largely by Mike WiLL Made-It, this album defines the energetic, "party" trap sound with high-pitched melodies and stadium-sized hooks. SremmLife 2 is another crucial release. |
| Meek Mill | Dreams and Nightmares | 2012 | Combining the grit of Philadelphia with Atlanta's trap production, this album features the high-octane "shouting" flow that became a staple of aggressive trap. Championships is his other modern classic. |
| Yo Gotti | I Am | 2013 | A veteran of the Memphis scene, this album showcases the "hustler" trap aesthetic with steady flows and heavy, brassy production. The Art of Hustle and CM10: Free Game are also significant. |
| Lil Yachty | Lil Boat | 2016 | This mixtape introduced "bubblegum trap," a playful, brightly melodic sub-genre that used trap rhythms with whimsical, lo-fi synths. Teenage Emotions and Let's Start Here (though different stylistically) are also notable. |
| A Boogie wit da Hoodie | Hoodie SZN | 2018 | This album represents the New York "melodic" trap sound, heavily influenced by R&B melodies but maintained by classic trap percussion. The Bigger Artist is his other essential project. |
| Rich The Kid | The World Is Yours | 2018 | Known for a frantic, "plug" influenced style, this album features high-tempo trap beats and a focus on flex-heavy lyricism. Rich Forever 3 (a collab mixtape) is also highly influential. |
| Polo G | Die a Legend | 2019 | This album defines the "melodic drill-trap" hybrid, featuring emotive piano melodies and intense, street-oriented storytelling. The Goat is his other definitive work. |
| Don Toliver | Heaven or Hell | 2020 | A showcase of the "spacey," melodic side of trap, utilizing heavy vocal processing and atmospheric, synth-driven production. Life of a Don is another essential atmospheric listen. |
| Moneybagg Yo | Time Served | 2020 | Representing the modern Memphis trap sound, this album focuses on a heavy, staccato delivery over dark, menacing trap beats. A Gangsta's Pain is his other chart-topping essential. |
