# Future Bass

Future Bass is a subgenre of electronic dance music that emerged in the early 2010s from the experimental bass music scenes of the UK and Australia, characterized by its lush, heavily modulated synthesizer chords, syncopated rhythms, and emotive, often pitch-shifted vocal chops. The genre's signature sound revolves around "wonky" supersaw and wavetable synth patches that swell and flutter through complex LFO modulations, layered over punchy, trap-influenced percussion with crisp snares and rolling hi-hats. Pioneered by producers like Flume, Hudson Mohawke, and Rustie, Future Bass blends elements of UK garage, trap, synth-pop, and chillwave into a colorful, high-energy sonic palette that ranges from euphoric festival anthems to introspective, downtempo pieces. Key production techniques include aggressive sidechain compression, granular vocal processing, and the use of bright, detuned chord stacks that create the genre's distinctively shimmering, "liquid" texture. The style gained massive mainstream traction by the mid-2010s through artists like Odesza, Illenium, and San Holo, who pushed it toward melodic pop crossover territory while maintaining its electronic core. Future Bass distinguishes itself from other bass music by prioritizing melody, emotional resonance, and colorful harmonic richness over raw sub-bass weight or rhythmic aggression.

| Flume | Skin | 2016 | This landmark album solidified the Future Bass sound with its signature "wonky" synth modulations, off-kilter rhythms, and cinematic sound design. | Illenium | Ashes | 2016 | A cornerstone of the melodic side of the genre, featuring soaring saw-wave chords, emotive vocal performances, and heavy percussion. This album helped bridge the gap between dubstep and melodic bass; his follow-ups Awake and Ascend further refined this anthemic style. |
| San Holo | album1 | 2018 | Defined by "bright" guitar melodies and shimmering, uplifting synth work, this record showcases the indie-electronic crossover potential of the genre. It emphasizes atmosphere and organic instrumentation over raw club power; his earlier New Sky EP is also a vital contribution. |
| Rustie | Glass Swords | 2011 | A pioneering release that laid the groundwork for Future Bass with its maximalist "aquajazz" aesthetic, featuring neon-bright synths and hip-hop influenced percussion. It introduced the hyper-melodic, high-energy palette that would define the movement; Green Language is another key work. |
| Hudson Mohawke | Butter | 2009 | An early blueprint for the genre, blending heavy sub-bass with candy-colored, sugar-rush synthesizers and soul-inflected vocal chops. This record established the "wonky" rhythmic swing that Flume and others would later popularize; the Lantern LP continued this evolution. |
| Wave Racer | Flash Drive EP | 2015 | Known for its hyper-active, 8-bit inspired melodies and tropical, bubbly atmosphere that epitomizes the "kawaii" side of Future Bass. The use of playful samples and high-pitched vocal flips became a staple of the Australian scene; his debut album To Isolate is also notable. |
| Odesza | In Return | 2014 | This album mastered the use of chopped vocal melodies and lush, atmospheric layers to create a sophisticated, accessible version of Future Bass. Its blend of live instrumentation and electronic beats set a high standard for the genre's production; A Moment Apart is equally influential. |
| What So Not | Not All the Beautiful Things | 2018 | Combining gritty, industrial textures with melodic hooks, this record explores the darker and more experimental corners of the genre. The intricate percussion and heavy "wall of sound" synths are hallmarks of the project's unique style; the Gemini EP remains a classic of the era. |
| Kasbo | Places We Don't Know | 2018 | A masterclass in "dreamy" Future Bass, focusing on evocative soundscapes, delicate vocal textures, and melancholic yet hopeful chord progressions. It leans into the cinematic side of the genre, prioritizing emotional resonance; his follow-up The Making of a Pariah is also essential. |
| Lido | Everything | 2016 | Highly influential for its complex, jazz-influenced chord structures and "stuttering" rhythmic arrangements that push the genre's technical boundaries. It treats Future Bass as a medium for high-concept pop storytelling; his I O U EPs are also highly regarded. |
| Petit Biscuit | Presence | 2017 | Characterized by its minimalist, airy production and the use of acoustic guitar loops paired with soft, side-chained synth pads. This album represents the more mellow, sunset-vibed "chill" Future Bass sound; his Parachute LP explored similar melodic territory. |
| Mura Masa | Someday Somewhere EP | 2015 | A pivotal release that blended UK garage influences with the budding Future Bass aesthetic, featuring steel drums, harps, and soulful vocal samples. It helped define the genre's penchant for organic, world-music-inspired textures; his Soundtrack to a Death mixtape is also vital. |
| Louis The Child | Kids at Play | 2018 | Exemplifies the "happy" and playful side of the genre with bright, bouncy synth stabs and catchy, pop-centric vocal collaborations. Their sound is defined by simplicity and infectious energy; the Love Is Alive EP is another definitive example of their style. |
| Said The Sky | Wide-Eyed | 2018 | A quintessential melodic Future Bass album that utilizes piano-driven melodies and soaring acoustic-electric arrangements to evoke deep nostalgia. It is widely praised for its high emotional impact and polished production; Sentiment is another major release in this vein. |
| Alison Wonderland | Awake | 2018 | This album fuses heavy trap-style percussion with shimmering, melodic Future Bass drops and raw, personal songwriting. It showcases the genre's ability to handle high-octane club energy alongside vulnerable pop hooks; her debut Run is also a cornerstone. |
| Ekali | A World Away | 2019 | Demonstrates a sophisticated balance between heavy, distorted bass and lush, ethereal melodies that create a sense of vast space. His production style is noted for its clarity and intricate layering; the Crystal Eyes EP also showcases his genre-defining sound. |
| Jai Wolf | The Cure to Loneliness | 2019 | Blending synth-pop with Future Bass, this album features shimmering synth washes and indie-rock-inspired song structures. It focuses on large-scale atmospheric builds and cinematic climaxes; his Kindred Spirits EP contains the seminal track "Indian Summer." |
| Whethan | Fantasy | 2020 | A high-energy take on the genre that incorporates alt-rock and pop elements through fuzzy synth textures and driving rhythms. This record highlights the genre's evolution into a more guitar-heavy, "crossover" sound; his Life of a Wallflower Vol. 1 is also essential. |
| Haywyre | Two Fold Pt. 1 | 2014 | Notable for blending virtuoso jazz piano with glitchy Future Bass production, featuring intricate "living" synths that follow complex harmonic movements. This record brought a higher level of musicality to the genre; Two Fold Pt. 2 further expanded on this fusion. |
| Cashmere Cat | 9 | 2017 | A master of the "minimalist" Future Bass sound, using sharp, plucky synths and innovative vocal processing to create a futuristic pop landscape. His use of space and silence is as impactful as his melodic drops; the Wedding Bells EP is a foundational genre text. |
| Grant | Wishes | 2019 | Combining nostalgic pop-punk energy with modern Future Bass production, this artist uses bright, overdriven synths and rapid-fire drum patterns. His work is known for its high melodic density and emotive themes; his earlier work as Grant Bowtie is also significant. |
| DROELOE | A Moment In Time EP | 2017 | Unique for their use of percussive textures — often sounding like clocks or chimes — and complex, dark-to-light melodic shifts. They brought a distinct, artistic visual-sonic aesthetic to the genre; their The Choices We Make EP is also a landmark. |
| Shallou | Magical Thinking | 2020 | Represents the "ambient" Future Bass niche, featuring slowed-down tempos, field recordings, and washed-out synth pads that create a sense of natural beauty. It is highly melodic but prioritizes a "calm" atmosphere; his Souls EP is another core release. |
| Gryffin | Gravity | 2019 | Fuses house-leaning rhythms with melodic Future Bass drops, often utilizing electric guitar riffs to anchor the tracks. This album is a prime example of the genre's ability to dominate main-stage festival sets; his Heading Home EP is also foundational. |
| Taska Black | MINDS EP | 2018 | Features a dark, moody approach to the genre with heavy use of strings and powerful, distorted basslines paired with haunting vocals. His sound is defined by its dramatic, orchestral scale; his singles like "Sandpaper" further define this style. |
| Slushii | Out of Light | 2017 | A high-octane blend of dubstep-influenced growls and sugary-sweet Future Bass melodies, often featuring his own processed vocals. This album captures the "colorful" and frenetic energy of the mid-2010s scene; Dream is a more ambient counterpart. |
| Slow Magic | Float | 2017 | Blends tribal, live-drum-heavy percussion with shimmering synth-pop and Future Bass textures to create an organic, otherworldly feel. The focus is on rhythmic drive and ethereal atmosphere; his album How To Run Away is also a genre essential. |
| Medasin | IRENE | 2018 | Defined by its "organic" and jazzy sound design, utilizing crisp percussion and lush, Rhodes-style keys under soft, modulated synths. This album pushed the genre toward a more "lo-fi" and sophisticated aesthetic; his Ripstick EP is also highly influential. |
| Dabin | Wild Youth | 2019 | Similar to Illenium, Dabin uses melodic guitar solos and massive synth drops to create a rock-infused Future Bass sound. The album is highly emotive and focuses on "storytelling" through its arrangements; Between Broken is another key release. |
| Hermitude | Dark Night Sweet Light | 2015 | This Australian duo blended hip-hop swagger with the emerging Future Bass palette, featuring heavy swing and bright, brassy synth leads. It represents the "bounce" side of the genre; their album Mirror Mountain continues this trajectory. |
| KRANE | Fallout | 2017 | Known for a "heavy" yet melodic style that uses sharp, cinematic percussion and orchestral elements to bolster wide, sweeping synth drops. This album showcases the genre's more aggressive, trap-influenced side; his SESSIONS series is also highly regarded. |
| Point Point | Family Portrait | 2016 | This French collective introduced a quirky, avant-garde sensibility to the genre, with sudden tempo changes and unconventional melodic choices. Their sound is characterized by "glitchy" elegance; the Contrast EP is another essential listen. |
| NGHTMRE | NGHTMRE - EP | 2016 | While often leaning into trap, this EP was instrumental in defining the "hard" Future Bass sound with its bright, brassy leads and aggressive rhythmic structures. | Seven Lions | Where I Won't Be Found EP | 2017 | A pioneer of "Melodic Bass," this release blends trance-like atmospheres with heavy Future Bass drops and soaring vocals. | Vanic | Here & Now | 2021 | Known for high-energy, pop-oriented Future Bass with massive, shimmering synth chords and catchy vocal hooks. This album collects the signature sound that made him a staple of the 2010s remix scene; his early remixes are foundational to the genre's rise. |
| Ganz | GAO EP | 2016 | This artist is credited with pioneering the "hyper-colored" Future Bass flip, using massive, saturating synth chords that drown out the mix in a melodic wash. His style is explosive and highly rhythmic; the Purple Noise EP is another key work. |
