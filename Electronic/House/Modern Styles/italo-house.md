# Italo House

Italo house is a subgenre of house music that emerged in the late 1980s from Italy, characterized by its bright, euphoric piano riffs — most iconically produced on the Korg M1 synthesizer — powerful diva vocals, and a polished, melodic production style that bridged the raw energy of Chicago house with European pop sensibility. The sound is built around uplifting chord progressions, driving four-on-the-floor rhythms, and lush string pads that create a sun-drenched, Mediterranean atmosphere, ranging from the high-energy piano-house anthems of the Media Records stable to the dreamy, ambient-tinged productions that would evolve into dream house. Pioneered by producers like Gianfranco Bortolotti, the Visnadi brothers, and artists on labels like Irma Records and UMM, Italo house drew from Italo disco's melodic traditions while incorporating the rhythmic framework and club functionality of American house music. The genre distinguishes itself from Chicago house through its emphasis on melody, vocal hooks, and studio polish, and from Eurodance through its deeper rhythmic foundations and club-oriented rather than pop-oriented production values. Italo house was instrumental in bringing house music to mainstream European audiences and directly spawned the dream house, Euro house, and vocal house movements of the 1990s.
