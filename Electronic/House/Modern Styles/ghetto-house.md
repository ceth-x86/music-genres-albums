# Ghetto House

Ghetto house is a raw, stripped-back subgenre of house music that emerged from Chicago's South Side in the late 1980s and early 1990s, defined by its minimalist production, relentless 808 and 909 drum machine patterns, and often explicit, repetitive vocal loops. Pioneered by producers on the Dance Mania label, the sound prioritizes physical, dancefloor-focused energy over melodic sophistication, using simple synth stabs, heavy kick drums, and chopped vocal samples to create a hypnotic, high-BPM groove. The genre is closely related to juke and footwork, which evolved from its increasingly rapid tempos and complex percussion patterns. Ghetto house distinguishes itself from mainstream Chicago house through its DIY production aesthetic, street-level lyrical content, and utilitarian approach to track-making designed specifically for DJ tools and club play. The sound spread beyond Chicago to influence Detroit's ghettotech scene, French touch producers, and various international electronic music movements, demonstrating the global reach of its raw rhythmic blueprint.
