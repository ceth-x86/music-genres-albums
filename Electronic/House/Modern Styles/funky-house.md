# Funky House

Funky house is a subgenre of house music that emerged in the mid-1990s, primarily from the French Touch and UK club scenes, built around heavily filtered disco and funk samples, side-chained compression, and infectious basslines that prioritize groove and dancefloor energy above all else. The sound is characterized by looping disco-funk hooks, soulful vocal samples, Latin percussion accents, shimmering synthesizers, and a polished production aesthetic that bridges the gap between classic 1970s funk and modern electronic dance music. Pioneered by producers like Armand Van Helden, Daft Punk, Masters At Work, and the French Touch movement, funky house drew its musical DNA directly from disco, soul, and funk records, recontextualizing them through house music's four-on-the-floor framework and contemporary filtering techniques. The genre distinguishes itself from deep house through its brighter, more energetic disposition and overt disco influences, and from tech house through its emphasis on melody, vocals, and rhythmic funk rather than minimalist repetition. Funky house dominated European club culture through the late 1990s and 2000s, spawning a radio-friendly vocal house variant that brought soulful dancefloor anthems into the pop mainstream.
