# Lo-Fi House

Lo-fi house is a subgenre of house music that emerged in the mid-2010s, characterized by its deliberate use of tape saturation, vinyl crackle, analog warmth, and degraded audio textures to evoke a sense of nostalgia and emotional intimacy. The sound draws from deep house, disco, and garage traditions but filters them through heavy compression, overdriven drum machines, and muffled frequency ranges that give tracks a worn, dusty quality reminiscent of old cassette recordings. Pioneered by artists on labels like Lobster Theremin, Shall Not Fade, and Unknown to the Unknown, lo-fi house rose to prominence through platforms like YouTube and Boiler Room, becoming synonymous with a "sad boy" aesthetic of wistful melodies and bittersweet vocal samples. The genre distinguishes itself from other house subgenres through its rejection of high-fidelity production standards, embracing imperfection and analog artifacts as core aesthetic choices rather than limitations. Lo-fi house spans a wide spectrum from club-ready tracks with punchy distorted kicks to ambient, bedroom-listening pieces that blur the line between house music and sound art.
