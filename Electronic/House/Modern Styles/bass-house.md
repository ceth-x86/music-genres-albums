# Bass House

Bass house is a high-energy subgenre of house music that merges the four-on-the-floor rhythmic foundation of house with the aggressive, heavily distorted basslines and sound design techniques borrowed from dubstep and UK bass music. Emerging in the mid-2010s through labels like Night Bass and Confession, the genre is characterized by metallic, wobbling mid-range synths, punchy kicks, and growling low-end frequencies that dominate the mix. Production often features sharp, rhythmic leads layered over tight, UK garage-inspired percussion and vocal chops. Bass house distinguishes itself from traditional house through its emphasis on sheer bass weight and drop-oriented song structures, while maintaining the danceable 128 BPM tempo range. The sound ranges from dark, industrial-tinged grooves to bright, chirpy synth hooks, with strong influences from G-house, jackin' house, and French electro.
