# Tropical House

Tropical house is a subgenre of house music that emerged in the early-to-mid 2010s, characterized by its warm, sun-drenched production aesthetic built around bright plucked synthesizer leads — often imitating pan flutes, marimbas, and steel drums — gentle kick drums, relaxed tempos around 100-115 BPM, and breathy, emotional vocal performances that evoke a perpetual summer atmosphere. The sound draws from deep house's laid-back groove and melodic sensibility while incorporating organic instrumentation like acoustic guitars, saxophones, and live percussion alongside the airy, reverb-heavy electronic textures that give the genre its distinctive "resort" quality. Popularized by Norwegian producer Kygo and further defined by artists like Thomas Jack, Robin Schulz, and Lost Frequencies, tropical house emerged as a softer, more accessible alternative to the aggressive drops of mainstream EDM, finding massive commercial success through streaming platforms and festival circuits. The genre distinguishes itself from deep house through its brighter melodic palette and pop-oriented song structures, and from standard EDM through its emphasis on atmosphere and warmth rather than build-and-drop dynamics. Tropical house became one of the most commercially dominant electronic subgenres of the mid-2010s, influencing mainstream pop production and spawning crossover hits that brought house music aesthetics to a global pop audience.
