# Soulful House

Soulful house is a subgenre of house music that places gospel-inspired vocals, live instrumentation, and warm, organic production at the center of its identity, creating an uplifting and emotionally resonant dancefloor experience. Emerging from the New York City club scene in the early 1990s through producers like Masters At Work and labels like King Street Sounds, the genre draws heavily from disco, classic soul, and jazz-funk traditions. The sound is characterized by lush Rhodes piano chords, live horn and string arrangements, rich vocal performances ranging from powerhouse gospel divas to intimate R&B crooners, and a deep, driving four-on-the-floor rhythm. Soulful house distinguishes itself from deep house through its emphasis on song structure and vocal performance over atmospheric minimalism, and from commercial dance-pop through its commitment to musical sophistication and jazz-influenced harmonic complexity. The genre has developed regional variations from the Latin-infused New York sound to the broken-beat-adjacent UK productions and the Afro-soul movement in South Africa, all united by a reverence for the spiritual and emotional power of soul music.
