| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Emperor | In the Nightside Eclipse | 1994 | This album set the blueprint for the genre by blending raw, freezing black metal riffs with sophisticated, layered keyboard arrangements that create a vast, cosmic atmosphere. It balances chaotic speed with regal, melodic grandeur, a feat they also explored on the equally essential Anthems to the Welkin at Dusk. |
| Dimmu Borgir | Enthrone Darkness Triumphant | 1997 | Moving away from their raw roots, this record introduced a polished, melodic, and cinematic production style that brought the genre to a wider audience. It features prominent, hook-driven synthesizer melodies and tight, rhythmic precision; other notable works include Stormblåst and Death Cult Armageddon. |
| Cradle of Filth | Dusk and Her Embrace | 1996 | Combining Gothic horror aesthetics with high-speed black metal, this album is defined by Dani Filth's extreme vocal range and complex, Victorian-inspired keyboard orchestrations. It leans heavily into a theatrical, narrative song structure, seen also on their landmark Cruelty and the Beast. |
| Limbonic Art | Moon in the Scorpio | 1996 | This duo utilized a drum machine and massive, swirling layers of symphonic synths to create an ethereal and hauntingly orchestral soundscape. The album is known for its long, epic compositions and a sense of "cosmic" dread, which continued on In Abhorrence Dementia. |
| Obtained Enslavement | Witchcraft | 1997 | Highlighting a strong neoclassical influence, this album features intricate, harpsichord-like synth lines that weave through technical and melodic guitar work. It is celebrated for its elegant yet aggressive atmosphere, also found on their follow-up, Soulblight. |
| Arcturus | Aspera Hiems Symfonia | 1996 | Serving as a bridge between cold black metal and avant-garde exploration, this album uses swirling, whimsical keyboards to create a frosty, stargazing atmosphere. It remains their most genre-pure release before they moved into weirder territory on La Masquerade Infernale. |
| Satyricon | Nemesis Divina | 1996 | While maintaining a fierce, traditional black metal edge, the inclusion of epic synthesizers and medieval-style melodies on tracks like "Mother North" defined the genre's "epic" side. It represents their peak symphonic period before transitioning to "black 'n' roll," alongside The Shadowthrone. |
| Borknagar | Borknagar | 1996 | This self-titled debut emphasizes a rugged, folk-tinged symphonic sound, utilizing epic melodies to evoke the majesty of the Norwegian landscape. It features a cleaner, more melodic approach to songwriting that they would further refine on The Olden Domain. |
| Bal-Sagoth | Starfire Burning Upon the Ice-Veiled Thrones of Ultima Thule | 1996 | Famous for their hyper-theatrical "fantasy-core" style, this album uses bombastic, film-score-style keyboards and spoken-word narration to tell intricate cosmic tales. Their sound is uniquely maximalist, a trait sustained across Battle Magic and The Power Cosmic. |
| Odium | The Sad Realm of the Lofty Ones | 1998 | A cult classic that focuses on extremely melodic, sorrowful keyboard motifs layered over high-speed, melancholic black metal. The production emphasizes the "wall of sound" created by the synths, making it a definitive example of the late '90s underground symphonic sound. |
| Graveworm | As the Angels Reach the Beauty | 1999 | This Italian band fused soaring, tragic melodies with heavy, mid-tempo riffs and deep growls, leaning into the more "romantic" side of the genre. Their use of woodwind-style synth patches and dark atmospheres is also prominent on When Daylight's Gone. |
| Covenant | Nexus Polaris | 1998 | Featuring a "supergroup" lineup, this album is characterized by its space-themed atmosphere and heavy use of melodic, futuristic synthesizers. It is more polished and accessible than contemporary releases, standing as a polished pillar of the genre's peak years. |
| Anorexia Nervosa | Drudenhaus | 2000 | This French band delivered a frantic, high-BPM version of the genre characterized by "baroque" levels of symphonic density and operatic intensity. It is an overwhelming, violent orchestral experience, a sound they perfected further on New Obscurantis Order. |
| Tidfall | Circular Studies | 2000 | Blending sleek, sci-fi aesthetics with aggressive symphonic black metal, this album utilizes "cold" digital synth textures to create a unique atmosphere. It represents the genre's turn toward a more modern, technical sound at the start of the millennium. |
| Ancient | The Cainian Chronicle | 1996 | A conceptual work that uses theatrical keyboards and female guest vocals to create a vampiric, narrative atmosphere. It is more mid-tempo and atmospheric than their debut, Svartalvheim, focusing on cinematic storytelling. |
| Sirius | Aeons of Magick | 2000 | Drawing heavy influence from Emperor, this Portuguese band utilized complex, celestial synth arrangements to create a fast-paced "astral" black metal sound. Their follow-up, Spectral Transition, is also a high-water mark for the symphonic style. |
| Illnath | Cast into Fields of Evil Pleasure | 2003 | This album features bouncy, melodic keyboard leads and a clean production style that borders on melodic death metal but retains the black metal vocal rasp. It represents the genre's evolution into more rhythmic and "catchy" territory. |
| Tartaros | The Red Jewel | 1999 | A one-man project that emphasizes grand, piano-driven melodies and dark, operatic arrangements. The album feels like a malevolent film score, utilizing a distinct "haunted mansion" aesthetic also found on the EP Heritage from the Past. |
| Hecate Enthroned | The Slaughter of Innocence, a Requiem for the Mighty | 1997 | Often compared to early Cradle of Filth, this album features high-pitched shrieks and extremely prominent, church-organ-style keyboards. It captures the essence of the mid-90s UK scene's obsession with gothic imagery and symphonic bombast. |
| Bishop of Hexen | Archives of an Enchanted Philosophy | 1997 | This Israeli band created a highly melodic and atmosphere-driven sound using unique, flute-like synth patches and folk-inspired melodies. It is a whimsical yet dark interpretation of the genre, continued on The Night-Sullen Appearance. |
| Mactatus | Provenance of Fear | 1998 | A quintessential example of the "second wave" of symphonic bands, featuring soaring synth melodies and a focus on epic, mid-paced compositions. They balanced melodicism with a raw Norwegian edge, as heard on Blot. |
| Vesperian Sorrow | Beyond the Realm of Phosphorescence | 1999 | Hailing from Texas, this band produced a surprisingly "European" sound with vast, sweeping keyboard soundscapes and technical drumming. Their style is characterized by a "galactic" and mournful atmosphere, also found on Psychotic Sculpture. |
| Carach Angren | Lammendam | 2008 | As leaders of the "modern" symphonic wave, they focus on "horror metal" with incredibly dense, realistic orchestral arrangements that follow a linear ghost-story narrative. Their theatricality is further explored on Death Came Through a Phantom Ship. |
| Seth | Les Blessures de l'Âme | 1998 | This French classic blends aggressive, traditional black metal with elegant, melancholic synth melodies. It is praised for its poetic atmosphere and sophisticated songwriting, which the band revisited on their 2021 return La Morsure du Christ. |
| Stormlord | At the Gates of Utopia | 2001 | Fusing "Extreme Epic Metal" with symphonic black metal, this Italian band uses bombastic, heroic synth lines and mythological themes. Their sound is more "triumphant" than "bleak," a style also found on Supreme Art of War. |
| Abigail Williams | In the Shadow of a Thousand Suns | 2008 | This album brought the symphonic sound to a new generation by mixing it with modern production and technical riffing. It features massive, cinematic orchestral backdrops, though the band later moved toward a darker, post-black style on Becoming. |
| Mystic Circle | Drachenblut | 1998 | A German take on the genre that emphasizes Wagnerian bombast and melodic "battle metal" keyboards. They leaned heavily into the campy, theatrical side of the genre, also evidenced on Infernal Satanic Verses. |
| Darzamat | Oniriad | 2003 | This Polish band utilized a mix of male and female vocals alongside lush, gothic-tinged symphonic arrangements. Their sound is atmospheric and slightly slower than their Norwegian counterparts, creating a "dreamlike" state also present on Transmition. |
| Evol | The Dream of the Formless | 1995 | An early, experimental take on the genre featuring medieval instrumentation like flutes and heavy use of atmospheric organ. It is more "atmospheric" than "symphonic" in the modern sense but was crucial in defining the genre's penchant for ancient aesthetics. |
| Kaldrad | Border of Ice | 1999 | Representing the "Blazebirth Hall" scene, this uses repetitive, hypnotic symphonic elements to create a cold, nationalistic, and atmospheric sound. It is much rawer and more lo-fi than the commercial symphonic bands, focusing on a grim, wintry aura. |
