| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Alcest | Ecailles de lune | 2010 | Neige pioneered the genre by blending the tremolo picking of black metal with the lush, ethereal textures of shoegaze. This album perfectly balances harsh vocals with nostalgic, dream-like melodies; other essential works include Souvenirs d'un autre monde and Shelter. |
| Deafheaven | Sunbather | 2013 | This record brought the genre to the mainstream by mixing aggressive blast beats and caustic shrieks with expansive, bright post-rock crescendos. Its "pink" aesthetic and wall-of-sound production are definitive; their later evolution is captured in New Bermuda and Infinite Granite. |
| Lantlos | .neon | 2010 | Featuring Neige on vocals, this album introduces a distinct urban, jazzy influence to the blackgaze template, creating a cold yet sophisticated atmosphere. The interplay between dissonant riffs and clean, melodic interludes is masterful; Agape is another highly regarded entry. |
| Amesoeurs | Amesoeurs | 2009 | Combining post-punk gloom with black metal intensity, this self-titled release captures a unique "industrial city" melancholy. The female-led clean vocals contrasted with raw screams set a high bar for the subgenre; the Ruines Humaines EP is also foundational. |
| Mol | Jord | 2018 | This Danish band offers a high-production, high-energy take on the sound, characterized by soaring leads and exceptionally clean transitions between fury and beauty. The album's rhythmic precision and vibrant atmosphere define the modern standard; Diorama serves as a strong follow-up. |
| Sadness | I Want to Be There | 2019 | A prolific solo project that leans heavily into the "dream" aspect of the genre, utilizing lo-fi production to create an overwhelming sense of nostalgia and grief. The music is characterized by shimmering synth layers and buried, emotional vocals; Leave is another standout title. |
| Holy Fawn | Death Spells | 2018 | Often described as "loud rock," this album emphasizes heavy, fuzzed-out shoegaze textures layered with blackened vocal outbursts and nature-inspired themes. The atmosphere is dense and immersive, blending doom and post-rock elements; Dimensional Bleed further explores this sound. |
| Heretoir | Heretoir | 2011 | This German project excels at the melancholic side of blackgaze, focusing on acoustic passages that explode into powerful, sweeping metal movements. The emotional weight and focus on isolation are central themes; The Circle is another essential part of their discography. |
| Numenorean | Adore | 2019 | This album features a highly polished sound that integrates progressive structures with traditional blackgaze contrasts. The melodic hooks are exceptionally strong, providing a sense of triumph amidst the underlying darkness; their debut Home offers a much bleaker perspective. |
| Astronoid | Air | 2016 | Often called "dream thrash," they strip away the harsh vocals entirely while keeping the blast beats and tremolo picking, creating an uplifting, celestial experience. It represents the "white" end of the blackgaze spectrum; their Self-Titled album continues this radiant style. |
| Sylvaine | Atoms Aligned, Coming Undone | 2018 | Multi-instrumentalist Kathrine Shepard crafts a sound deeply rooted in spiritual atmosphere, utilizing her ethereal clean voice alongside visceral screams. The music moves between delicate folk-inspired passages and dense blackened shoegaze; Wistful is also highly recommended. |
| Show Me a Dinosaur | Plantgazer | 2020 | A Russian ensemble that emphasizes the "wall of sound" technique, using layers of reverb-drenched guitars to create a massive, overwhelming sonic environment. The transitions between serene post-rock and aggressive metal are seamless and organic; their Self-Titled debut is equally potent. |
| Unreqvited | Mosaic I: L'amour et l'ardeur | 2018 | This project leans toward the symphonic and "depressive" side of the genre, featuring high-pitched shrieks over beautiful, orchestral-tinged melodies. It creates a feeling of vast, lonely landscapes; Disaffected is another key release in the project's large catalog. |
| Violet Cold | Anomie | 2017 | Known for incorporating unconventional elements like electronic beats and traditional folk instruments, this Azerbaijani project pushes the genre's boundaries. The sound remains anchored by soaring blackgaze melodies and intense energy; Kosmos is another experimental highlight. |
| An Autumn for Crippled Children | Lost | 2010 | This Dutch band focuses on a lo-fi, synth-heavy aesthetic that feels deeply rooted in 80s post-punk and new wave. The production is intentionally hazy, making the melodies feel like distant memories; The Seal of Every World is another definitive work. |
| White Ward | Love Exchange Failure | 2019 | While they are known for "blackjazz," this album uses the blackgaze foundation to explore urban noir themes through saxophone and dark ambient textures. The interplay between the harsh metal and smooth jazz elements is unique and evocative; Futility Report is their other major work. |
| Ghost Bath | Moonlover | 2015 | This band utilizes incredibly high-pitched, wordless wails that function more as an instrument than traditional vocals, creating a unique sense of anguish. The music itself is often surprisingly melodic and major-key, following the "Sunbather" blueprint; Starmourner is also essential. |
| Harakiri for the Sky | III: Trauma | 2016 | Moving more toward the "post-black" side, this album is defined by its driving, melodic guitar leads and relentless rhythmic energy. It captures a specific sense of modern urban despair and burnout; Aokigahara is another landmark in their career. |
| Woods of Desolation | As the Stars | 2014 | This Australian project blends depressive black metal with uplifting, shoegaze-inspired guitar melodies to create a "bittersweet" sound. The atmosphere is profoundly emotional and nostalgic; Torn Beyond Reason is considered one of the genre's greatest achievements. |
| Bosse-de-Nage | All Events Transpire | 2015 | This band offers a more abrasive, math-rock influenced version of blackgaze, with a focus on frantic drumming and surreal, spoken-word-style vocals. It is a more intellectual and dissonant take on the genre's tropes; III is another key chapter. |
| Infant Island | Sepulcher | 2020 | Blending screamo and post-hardcore with blackgaze, this album is characterized by its chaotic energy and raw, vulnerable vocal performances. The shimmering guitar work provides a beautiful contrast to the frantic pace; their Self-Titled album is also highly influential. |
| Hopesfall | The Satellite Years | 2002 | A proto-blackgaze essential, this album merged melodic hardcore with space-rock and shoegaze textures long before the term was coined. Its influence on the atmospheric side of heavy music is immeasurable; Magnetic North is another important melodic release. |
| Kardashev | The Almanac | 2017 | Self-described as "prog-gaze," they incorporate death-metal growls and technical proficiency into a lush, atmospheric shoegaze framework. The production is crystal clear, emphasizing the scale of the soundscapes; The Baring of Shadows is another notable EP. |
| Planning for Burial | Below the House | 2017 | A one-man project that sits at the intersection of slowcore, doom, and blackgaze, characterized by heavy, repetitive riffs and muffled, distant vocals. The mood is one of crushing isolation and domestic gloom; Leaving is another essential, bleak listen. |
| Oathbreaker | Rheia | 2016 | While rooted in blackened hardcore, this album leans heavily into post-rock and shoegaze, utilizing clean singing and dynamic builds to great effect. It is a landmark in the "heavy-meets-ethereal" movement; Eros|Anteros is also essential. |
| Vattnet Viskar | Settler | 2015 | This album presents a more direct and aggressive style, focusing on rhythmic drive and soaring, "cosmic" guitar melodies. It avoids some of the genre's typical haziness in favor of punchy, clear execution; their debut Sky Swallower is also a strong entry. |
| Cold Body Lotus | The Earth Is Fine | 2022 | A newer example that emphasizes the "gaze" with walls of feedback and shimmering, multi-layered guitars over standard black metal rhythms. The atmosphere is overwhelmingly dense and colorful; their earlier demos established this signature thick sound. |
| Somn | The Next World | 2020 | This Russian band focuses on the "atmospheric" side, using long, evolving structures and a heavy emphasis on reverb-drenched leads. The sound is cold, distant, and deeply immersive; their EP Somn features a similarly expansive approach. |
| Underaiki | Black Clouds | 2021 | This project utilizes very traditional shoegaze vocal styles—hushed and melodic—against a backdrop of intense, blackened percussion. It captures the "dreamy" aspect of the genre with high fidelity; their previous singles paved the way for this cohesive sound. |
| Asunojokei | Island | 2022 | A Japanese band that breathes new life into the genre by adding math-rock complexity and J-pop melodic sensibilities to the blackened foundations. The result is a vibrant, chaotic, and uniquely bright take on the style; Awakening is another standout release. |
