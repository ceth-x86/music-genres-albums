# Deathcore

Deathcore fuses the technical brutality of death metal with the breakdown-driven structures of metalcore and hardcore punk, creating one of extreme music's most physically punishing hybrid genres. Emerging in the early-to-mid 2000s, largely through the internet and the MySpace era, the genre is defined by its use of blast beats alternating with slow, crushing breakdowns, guttural and pig-squeal vocals, and heavily down-tuned or drop-tuned guitars. The production style tends toward a modern, compressed sound that maximizes low-end impact, making the breakdowns feel seismically heavy. Lyrically, themes range from nihilistic violence and horror to anti-religious sentiment and personal anguish, often delivered through a combination of high shrieks and cavernous gutturals. Since its initial wave, the genre has splintered into numerous substyles including symphonic deathcore, blackened deathcore, and downtempo deathcore, each pushing the boundaries of extremity in different directions. Deathcore continues to evolve rapidly, with modern acts incorporating orchestral elements, electronic production, and progressive song structures while retaining the genre's signature visceral impact.

| Despised Icon | The Ills of Modern Man | 2007 | This album perfected the "dual-vocal" attack and technical bree-ing that became a genre staple. It seamlessly integrates blast beats with massive, calculated breakdowns. Other essential works include The Healing Process and Day of Mourning. |
| Suicide Silence | The Cleansing | 2007 | Widely considered the "Deathcore Bible," it features Mitch Lucker's iconic high-pitched screams and bone-crushing, simplistic breakdowns. It established the standard for the genre's aesthetics and raw production. Their later album No Time to Bleed is also fundamental. |
| Whitechapel | This Is Exile | 2008 | Utilizing a three-guitar assault, this album brought a layer of dark atmosphere and technical complexity to the genre's rhythm sections. Phil Bozeman's guttural depth set a new bar for vocalists. The Somatic Defilement and A New Era of Corruption are equally vital. |
| Job for a Cowboy | Doom (EP) | 2005 | Though an EP, it is the catalyst that brought deathcore to the mainstream via the internet, characterized by its "pig squeal" vocals and rapid-fire tempo shifts. This release defined the MySpace-era sound. They later pivoted toward technical death metal with Genesis. |
| The Acacia Strain | Continent | 2008 | This album leans into the slowest, heaviest aspects of the genre, prioritizing "the groove" and nihilistic lyrical themes over speed. It is a masterclass in down-tuned, sludge-influenced destruction. Wormwood and The Dead Walk are also essential listening. |
| Carnifex | Dead in My Arms | 2007 | Blending blackened atmospheric touches with unrelenting breakdown-heavy structures, this album defines the "no-frills" aggressive side of the genre. It focuses on bleakness and sheer velocity. The Diseased and the Poisoned and Hell Chose Me further cemented their legacy. |
| All Shall Perish | The Price of Existence | 2006 | This record introduced a high level of melodic shredding and European death metal influence into the deathcore framework. It proved the genre could be technically proficient and musically intricate. Awaken the Dreamers is another landmark title. |
| Chelsea Grin | Desolation of Eden | 2010 | Characterized by its high-tempo energy and "chug-heavy" breakdowns, this album epitomizes the second wave of deathcore's rise. It balances technical lead work with massive, rhythmic slams. Their self-titled EP and My Damnation are also cornerstone releases. |
| Thy Art Is Murder | Hate | 2012 | This album brought a polished, massive production style to the genre, emphasizing anti-religious themes and mechanical precision in the drumming. It helped launch Australian deathcore onto the global stage. Infinite Death and Holy War are also defining works. |
| Fit For An Autopsy | Hellbound | 2013 | Balancing technical proficiency with a dark, modern soundscape, this album showcased the genre's evolution into more structured and atmospheric territory. It features crushing low-end and intricate riffs. The Process of Enlightenment and Absolute Hope Absolute Hell are also notable. |
| The Red Chord | Clients | 2005 | A pioneer of the "grindcore-leaning" deathcore sound, this album features chaotic structures, dissonant chords, and unpredictable rhythmic shifts. It represents the genre's more experimental and frantic origins. Fused Together in Revolving Doors is their other essential masterwork. |
| Impending Doom | Nailed. Dead. Risen. | 2007 | Defining the "Gored-core" sound, this album utilized extremely distorted production and "grinding" vocal textures. It is known for its sheer sonic density and heavy religious (or "anti-Satanic") themes. The Serpent Servant is another key release. |
| Through the Eyes of the Dead | Bloodlust | 2005 | This album leans heavily into the melodic death metal side of the genre, featuring Swedish-style riffing combined with American hardcore breakdowns. It is a bridge between the two styles. Malice also stands as a definitive genre entry. |
| As Blood Runs Black | Allegiance | 2006 | Notable for its incredibly fast "gravity blasts" and melodic, catchy guitar hooks, this album is a prime example of high-energy melodic deathcore. It remains a fan favorite for its relentless pacing. Instinct is their other prominent contribution. |
| Oceano | Depths | 2009 | This album pushed the boundaries of vocal depth and "slow-motion" breakdowns, earning the band a reputation for being one of the heaviest in the scene. It focuses on suffocating atmosphere and low-tuned brutality. Contagion is another essential part of their discography. |
| Lorna Shore | Immortal | 2020 | Representing the "Blackened Deathcore" evolution, this album incorporates symphonic elements and extreme technicality into the core structure. It marked a shift toward cinematic and epic songwriting within the genre. Psalms and ...And I Return to Nothingness are also critical. |
| Shadow of Intent | Melancholy | 2019 | This album showcases the pinnacle of symphonic deathcore, blending soaring orchestrations with highly technical death metal riffing. The storytelling and vocal range are defining characteristics of the modern era. Reclaimer is their other standout masterwork. |
| Infant Annihilator | The Elysian Grandeval Galèriarch | 2016 | Pushing the genre to its absolute technical and sonic extremes, this album features hyper-speed drumming and exaggeratedly grotesque vocal performances. It is a benchmark for the "internet-born" technical deathcore movement. The Palpable Leprosy of Pollution is also essential. |
| Signs of the Swarm | Vital Depravity | 2017 | Known for some of the most "inhuman" vocal textures in the genre, this album focuses on slamming rhythms and a dark, oppressive atmosphere. It is a cornerstone of the modern "slam-deathcore" hybrid. Absolvere is another notable release. |
| Spite | Nothing is Beautiful | 2017 | This album defines the "angry," high-intensity side of the genre, focusing on raw emotion and chaotic, hardcore-influenced energy. The vocal delivery is exceptionally aggressive and varied. The Root of All Evil is another definitive title. |
| Angelmaker | Dissentient | 2015 | Featuring a dual-vocalist setup, this album captures the frantic energy of old-school deathcore while maintaining high technical standards. It is celebrated for its punishing speed and diverse vocal range. Their self-titled album is also a major genre staple. |
| Enterprise Earth | Luciferous | 2019 | Blending progressive structures with traditional deathcore brutality, this album highlights the genre's capacity for complex songwriting and technical guitar solos. It maintains a dark, heavy core throughout. Embodiment and The Chosen are also highly influential. |
| Black Tongue | Nadir | 2018 | This album is the definitive "Downtempo Deathcore" release, emphasizing agonizingly slow tempos and a crushing, doom-influenced weight. It creates a uniquely dark and nihilistic atmosphere. The Unconquerable Dark is their other essential offering. |
| Animosity | Empires | 2005 | A classic of the early Bay Area scene, this album features a heavy thrash influence and "spazzy," technical structures. It represents the genre before it became more formulaic. Animal is another foundational album for the band. |
| Molotov Solution | The Resurrection | 2009 | Combining political themes with a crushing, mid-tempo deathcore sound, this album is known for its tight execution and massive breakdowns. It helped define the more "serious" lyrical side of the genre. Insurrection is also a key record. |
| Beneath the Massacre | Evidence of Inequity (EP) | 2005 | While leaning toward technical death metal, this release's use of hyper-technical breakdowns and mathcore-like precision was hugely influential on deathcore's evolution. It is a masterclass in speed. Mechanics of Dysfunction is their defining full-length. |
| I Declare War | Malevolence | 2010 | This album is a staple of the "heavy-hitting" style, featuring some of the most recognizable and bone-shaking breakdowns of its era. It is straightforward, punishing, and rhythmically focused. Amidst the Bloodshed is another vital release. |
| Suffokate | No Mercy, No Forgiveness | 2010 | Blending a heavy "tough-guy" hardcore attitude with death metal speed, this album is a definitive example of the "hardcore-leaning" deathcore sound. The breakdowns are designed for maximum impact. Oakland is their other notable work. |
| Elysia | Masochist | 2008 | An early pioneer of the "angry" deathcore sound, featuring high-pitched shrieked vocals and raw, unpolished aggression. It was highly influential on the aesthetics of the mid-2000s scene. Their Moving Idols era showed a more experimental shift. |
| Knights of the Abyss | Juggernaut | 2007 | This album features a heavy emphasis on "galloping" riffs and melodic leads, typical of the early Arizona deathcore scene. It is a high-speed, aggressive staple of the genre's first peak. Shades is another essential record. |
| Rose Funeral | The Resting Silent | 2009 | A perfect blend of melodic death metal influence and crushing slams, this album is known for its dark themes and consistent intensity. It represents the "blackened" melodic side of the early scene. Gates of Punishment is also critical. |
| Salt the Wound | Carnal Repercussions | 2008 | This album is a prime example of the "melodic deathcore" style that was popular in the late 2000s, featuring dual-guitar harmonies and high-energy drumming. It is both catchy and brutal. Ares is their other major release. |
| The Last Ten Seconds of Life | Invivo [Exvivo] | 2013 | Pioneering a "grittier," more groove-oriented deathcore sound, this album incorporates elements of nu-metal and hardcore into its heavy structures. It is known for its unique rhythmic bounce. Soulless Hymns is another genre landmark. |
| BODYSNATCHER | This Heavy Void | 2020 | A leading name in the modern "downtempo" and "slam-influenced" deathcore scene, this album focuses on raw, visceral aggression and slow, punishing grooves. It is designed for maximum physical impact. Abandonment is also essential. |
| Brand of Sacrifice | Lifeblood | 2021 | Pushing the boundaries of electronic integration and "anime-inspired" intensity, this album features hyper-produced, massive choruses and technical riffs. It represents the modern, cinematic deathcore sound. God Hand is their other defining work. |
| To the Grave | Global Warning | 2019 | This album brought an environmentally conscious and highly aggressive "Australian" sound back to the forefront, featuring chaotic vocal patterns and crushing speed. It is relentlessly intense. Epilogue is another key compilation/release. |
| Mental Cruelty | A Hill to Die Upon | 2021 | A masterpiece of blackened deathcore, this album blends extreme speed, symphonic textures, and crushing slams with a high level of technicality. It is dark, epic, and precisely executed. Inferis is another standout title. |
| Slaughter to Prevail | Kostolom | 2021 | Defined by Alex Terrible's ultra-low gutturals and a mix of nu-metal groove with deathcore power, this album brought the genre to massive new audiences. It is characterized by its "tough" and anthemic sound. Chapters of Misery is also essential. |
| Misericordiam | Unbounded by the Chains of Prosecution | 2006 | An underground classic that leaned heavily into the "slam" side of deathcore before it was a common trope. It is raw, chaotic, and features extremely guttural vocals. A Thin Line Between Redemption and Ruin is also notable. |
| A Night in Texas | The God Delusion | 2015 | Known for its incredibly fast vocal delivery and anti-religious themes, this album is a high-speed assault on the senses. It represents the more technical and frantic side of the Australian scene. Global Slaughter is another essential record. |
| Hester Prynne | The Goswell Divorce | 2009 | This album is a cult classic known for its "Southern-fried" deathcore riffs and highly catchy, yet brutal, songwriting. It has a unique rhythmic swing not found in many other bands of the era. Black Heart Bearer is also key. |
| Upon a Burning Body | The World Is Ours | 2010 | Blending a heavy "Pantera-esque" groove with deathcore breakdowns, this album brought a party-centric and aggressive energy to the genre. It is rhythmically driving and anthemic. Red. White. Green. is another definitive work. |
| Winds of Plague | Decimate the Weak | 2008 | This album popularized the "symphonic" deathcore style, using keyboards to create an epic, cinematic atmosphere alongside hardcore-style gang vocals and breakdowns. It is a unique genre hybrid. The Great Stone War is also foundational. |
| Conducting from the Grave | When Legends Become Dust | 2009 | A highly technical and melodic album that emphasized guitar wizardry over pure "chugging." It remains a favorite for fans of the more sophisticated side of deathcore. Their self-titled album is also highly regarded. |
| Born of Osiris | The New Reign | 2007 | Though later evolving into progressive metalcore, this debut is a fundamental "technical deathcore" release, utilizing keyboards and complex syncopation. It defined the "Sumerian-core" sound. A Higher Place is another essential early work. |
| Veil of Maya | The Common Man's Collapse | 2008 | This album blended technical death metal with "mathy" rhythms and deathcore breakdowns, creating a highly influential, complex sound. It is a benchmark for technical precision. Id is also a cornerstone of their early sound. |
| Aversions Crown | Tyrant | 2014 | Focusing on "Aliencore" themes, this album uses eerie guitar effects and hyper-fast drumming to create a sci-fi, extraterrestrial atmosphere. It is cold, mechanical, and heavy. Xenocide is their other defining masterwork. |
| Vulvodynia | Praenuntius Infiniti | 2021 | A leading example of the "Slamming Deathcore" subgenre, this album features high-speed technicality mixed with massive, groove-heavy slams. It is sonically dense and varied. Psychosadistic Design is another essential release. |
| Distant | Aeons of Oblivion | 2021 | This album represents the "Downtempo/Slam" hybrid at its most massive, with a focus on world-building lore and crushing, slow-motion destruction. It is dark and incredibly heavy. Tyrannotophia is also critical. |
| Worm Shepherd | Ritual Hymns | 2022 | A modern pinnacle of blackened deathcore, featuring haunting melodies, symphonic depth, and some of the most diverse vocal performances in the current scene. It is atmospheric and punishing. In the Wake of Sol is their other notable entry. |
