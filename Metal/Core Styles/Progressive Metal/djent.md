| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Meshuggah | obZen | 2008 | As the architects of the sound, Meshuggah defines the genre through "Bleed," showcasing the signature high-gain, palm-muted polyrhythmic patterns and mechanical precision. Other essential works include Destroy Erase Improve and Nothing. |
| Periphery | Periphery II: This Time It's Personal | 2012 | This album solidified the modern "bulb" sound, blending complex 7-string syncopation with soaring melodic choruses and polished production. Misha Mansoor's work here and on their Self-Titled debut established the genre's standard. |
| Animals As Leaders | Animals as Leaders | 2009 | Tosin Abasi pioneered the instrumental side of the genre, utilizing 8-string guitars for thumb-thumping techniques and jazz-fusion inspired lead work. The Joy of Motion and Weightless are also pivotal instrumental milestones. |
| TesseracT | One | 2011 | Defining the "atmospheric" side of the branch, this album focuses on clean, ambient textures contrasted against heavy, elastic bass grooves and odd-time signatures. Their follow-ups Altered State and Sonder further refined this ethereal approach. |
| Vildhjarta | måsstaden | 2011 | This record introduced the "thall" sub-movement, characterized by extremely dark, dissonant chord voicings and jarring, stop-start rhythmic violence. The band's unique atmosphere is also captured in Thousands of Evils and måsstaden under vatten. |
| Monuments | Gnosis | 2012 | Highlighting the groove-oriented "bounce" of the genre, this album features intricate interlocking guitar and drum patterns with a heavy emphasis on staccato riffing. The Amanuensis and Phronesis are also core examples of their rhythmic style. |
| After The Burial | Rareform | 2008 | A bridge between metalcore and technical groove, this album utilized 8-string guitars early on to create crushing, low-tuned breakdowns and neo-classical leads. Dig Deep and In Dreams further showcase their high-energy rhythmic complexity. |
| Born of Osiris | The Discovery | 2011 | This album successfully integrated symphonic keyboards and shred-heavy leads into the staccato, low-end framework of the genre. The New Reign and Tomorrow We Die Alive are other significant entries in their discography. |
| Intervals | The Way Forward | 2017 | Originally a vocal project, Aaron Marshall's shift to purely instrumental music focused on bright, upbeat melodic phrasing and technical "chugging" motifs. In Time and Circadian are also highly influential in the instrumental scene. |
| Skyharbor | Guiding Lights | 2014 | This record emphasizes the progressive and cinematic side of the genre, using layered guitar soundscapes and intricate percussion to create a sense of scale. Blinding White Noise: Illusion and Chaos is another essential listen for their earlier, heavier sound. |
| Uneven Structure | Februus | 2011 | A masterclass in "ambient-djent," this concept album uses massive, wall-of-sound production and long-form compositions to explore rhythmic tension and release. Their later works La Partition and Paragon continue this dense, atmospheric exploration. |
| Cloudkicker | Beacons | 2010 | Ben Sharp's solo project helped define the DIY, bedroom-producer era of the genre with post-rock influences and math-metal rhythms. The Discovery and Fade are also landmark releases for independent instrumental metal. |
| Northlane | Singularity | 2013 | This album represents the "Northlane-core" style, characterized by spacey, reverb-drenched leads over technical, bouncy rhythmic foundations. Discoveries and Alien are also vital to the evolution of their sound. |
| Veil of Maya | Eclipse | 2012 | This release highlights a shorter, punchier song structure while maintaining high levels of technicality and syncopated, low-tuned riffs. ID and Matriarch are also definitive examples of their blend of technical deathcore and melodic grooves. |
| Volumes | Via | 2011 | Focusing on the "urban" or hip-hop influenced groove, this album features dual vocals and a heavy emphasis on the "bounce" of the rhythm section. No Sleep and Happier? are other key entries in their bouncy, melodic catalog. |
| Erra | Augment | 2013 | Balancing technicality with lush melody, this album features intricate tapping sequences and 7-string rhythmic complexity typical of the genre's progressive side. Impulse and their Self-Titled album are equally essential for this style. |
| The Contortionist | Exoplanet | 2010 | A landmark of "space-djent," blending heavy, syncopated deathcore elements with expansive, atmospheric synth passages and prog-metal transitions. Intrinsic and Language show their evolution into a more melodic direction. |
| Structures | Divided By | 2011 | This album pushed the genre toward more chaotic, frantic rhythmic patterns and dissonant "skronk" sounds, influencing the more aggressive side of the scene. Their EP All of the Above is also highly regarded for its raw energy. |
| Human Abstract | Digital Veil | 2011 | Blending neo-classical metal with the mechanical rhythms of the genre, this album features some of the most complex guitar interplay and counterpoint in the scene. Nocturne is another highly respected progressive work of theirs. |
| Plini | Handmade Cities | 2016 | Representing the softer, more "positive" side of the movement, Plini uses syncopated palm-muting as a foundation for intricate, virtuosic jazz-metal fusion. The End of Everything and Impulse Voices are other core instrumental works. |
| Chimp Spanner | At the Ebb and Flow of the Void | 2009 | Paul Ortiz defines the futuristic, sci-fi aesthetic of the genre with dense layers of synths and 8-string rhythmic precision. All Roads Lead Here is another essential example of this high-tech instrumental style. |
| Modern Day Babylon | Travelers | 2013 | This project specializes in "pure" instrumental groove, focusing heavily on the low-end "thump" and clean, staccato rhythmic execution. The Ocean Atlas and Coma are also essential for fans of the instrumental sound. |
| Heart of a Coward | Severance | 2013 | This album highlights the heavier, more aggressive "groove-metal" end of the spectrum with massive, low-tuned riffs and a focus on raw power. Hope and Hindrance and Deliverance are also definitive of their style. |
| Hacktivist | Outside the Box | 2016 | Combining the genre's signature syncopated riffs with grime-influenced rapping, this album created a unique crossover between electronic, hip-hop, and technical metal. Their Self-Titled EP is also foundational for this fusion. |
| SikTh | The Trees Are Dead & Dried Out Wait for Something Wild | 2003 | Though predating the term "djent," this album's chaotic time signatures and dual-vocal delivery heavily influenced the genre's technical and progressive leanings. Death of a Dead Day is also a critical influence on the entire movement. |
| Vitalism | Sy | 2017 | An instrumental powerhouse that blends Latin-influenced rhythms with high-gain, percussive guitar work and technical lead-shredding. Their EP Gradience further showcases their unique, high-energy take on the sound. |
| Polaris | The Mortal Coil | 2017 | This record perfectly blends the "bouncy" rhythmic elements of the genre with metalcore sensibilities and highly catchy, melodic hooks. The Death of Me and Fatalism continue their dominance in the modern heavy scene. |
| Kadinja | Ascendancy | 2017 | A standout in the French scene, this album features incredibly technical, "twanky" guitar riffs and odd-time signatures with a strong sense of melody. Super 90' and DNA are also essential listens for technical fans. |
| Destiny Potato | LUN | 2014 | Now known as Sordid Pink, this project combined ultra-heavy, complex rhythmic structures with pop-influenced female vocals and quirky electronic elements. This album remains a unique "cult classic" in the genre's history. |
| Novelists | Souvenirs | 2015 | This album showcases the "melodic-djent" style, utilizing technical lead work and syncopated grooves to support highly emotional and catchy vocal arrangements. Noir and C'est La Vie are also significant to their evolving sound. |
