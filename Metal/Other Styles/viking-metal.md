| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bathory | Hammerheart | 1990 | Transitioning from black metal to a majestic, mid-paced style, this album introduced the choral arrangements and Norse mythology themes that birthed the genre. It emphasizes atmosphere over speed, a template also seen in their follow-up Twilight of the Gods. |
| Enslaved | Frost | 1994 | This album perfectly balances icy black metal aggression with folk-inspired melodies and expansive, epic structures centered on Viking lore. The band's earlier Vikingligr Veldi is also a cornerstone, but Frost defines their core ancient aesthetic. |
| Einherjer | Dragons of the North | 1996 | Featuring heavy, galloping riffs and harsh vocals, this record captures the "warrior" spirit of the genre through its folk-inflected chord progressions and lyrical focus on sea voyages. Other notable works include the Leve Vikingånden EP. |
| Falkenbach | ...En because death-grey-absent... | 1996 | This solo project masterfully blends clean, hymnal vocals with raspy black metal snarls and prominent flute and keyboard melodies. The artist Vratyas Vakyas further refined this epic, solitary sound on The Magni Blandinn Ok Megintiri.... |
| Helheim | Av norrøn ætt | 1997 | Rooted in the Bergen scene, this album utilizes raw production and traditional instruments like the jaw harp to create a dark, ritualistic atmosphere. Their debut Jormundgand is equally essential for its primitive, cold Viking energy. |
| Hades Almighty | The Dawn of the Dying Sun | 1997 | Originally known simply as Hades, this band delivered a slow-burning, atmospheric take on the genre characterized by booming production and epic, drawn-out compositions. Their debut ...Again Shall Be is also a landmark of early Norwegian Viking metal. |
| Mithotyn | In the Sign of the Ravens | 1997 | This Swedish band emphasized melodic guitar harmonies and a "battle-ready" tempo that influenced much of the folk-metal crossover to follow. The albums King of the Distant Forest and Gathered Around the Kirk are also vital genre entries. |
| Windir | 1184 | 2001 | Valfar's masterpiece integrates Sognametal—a blend of folk melodies and black metal—with sophisticated electronic textures and traditional harmonies. The albums Sóknardalr and Arntor are also foundational to this specific regional sound. |
| Moonsorrow | Voimasta ja kunniasta | 2001 | Though they later leaned into progressive lengths, this album is a pure display of "Epic Heathen Metal" with massive choral layers and folk instrumentation. Kivenkantaja is another essential record that defines their cinematic approach. |
| Thyrfing | Vansinnesvisor | 2002 | This album moved away from standard "jolly" folk melodies toward a darker, more dissonant, and experimental sound that captures the grim reality of Viking life. Their self-titled debut and Valdr Galga are also highly regarded. |
| Månegarm | Dödsfärd | 2003 | This record highlights the "Viking-punk" energy, featuring upbeat rhythms, aggressive violin melodies, and a mix of clean and harsh vocals. Other notable titles in their discography include Vargstenen and Nordstjärnans tidsålder. |
| Kampfar | Fra underverdenen | 1999 | This album focuses on a "pagan" black metal sound that is deeply rhythmic and atmospheric, emphasizing the folklore of the Norwegian mountains and woods. Their earlier Mellom skogkledde aasene is another defining piece of folklore-infused metal. |
| Ensiferum | Ensiferum | 2001 | While leaning toward folk, their debut is a quintessential "heroic" metal album, blending fast power metal elements with harsh vocals and epic storytelling. Their second album, Iron, further solidified this high-energy Viking style. |
| Borknagar | The Olden Domain | 1997 | A landmark of "Northman" metal, this album features Garm's soaring clean vocals and complex, majestic arrangements that lean into the philosophical side of Norse history. Their self-titled debut is also an essential, rawer black-Viking hybrid. |
| Solefald | The Linear Scaffold | 1997 | This avant-garde take on the genre introduced unconventional structures and clean vocals while maintaining a deep connection to Nordic identity and art. Their Pills Against the Ageless Ills also explores these experimental Viking boundaries. |
| Týr | Eric the Red | 2003 | Hailing from the Faroe Islands, this band uses complex time signatures and traditional Faroese folk melodies delivered with powerful clean vocals and heavy riffs. How Far to Asgaard is another notable album exploring their island heritage. |
| Ereb Altor | By Honour | 2008 | Explicitly following the path laid by Bathory's epic era, this album features slow, heavy tempos, massive choirs, and a mournful, heroic atmosphere. The End is another key release that pays homage to the Quorthon-style Viking sound. |
| Graveland | Thousand Swords | 1995 | A pioneer of the "Pagan" branch, this album uses lo-fi production and folk-inspired keyboard melodies to evoke a medieval, warlike atmosphere. Creed of Iron is another notable record that moved further into symphonic Viking territories. |
| Scald | Will of the Gods Is Great Power | 1996 | A unique "Viking Doom" entry from Russia, this album features incredibly powerful operatic vocals and slow, crushing riffs that evoke the weight of ancient myths. It remains a singular, essential cult classic of the genre. |
| Storm | Nordavind | 1995 | A side project featuring members of Darkthrone and Satyricon, this album is a focused collection of traditional Norwegian folk songs reimagined with heavy, mid-paced metal arrangements. It is a defining document of nationalistic folk-Viking metal. |
| Wodensthrone | Loss | 2009 | While originating in the UK, this band captures the atmospheric side of the genre with long, sweeping compositions and a focus on ancestral sorrow and landscape. Their follow-up Curse is also highly praised for its depth and intensity. |
| Keep of Kalessin | Through Times of War | 1997 | Their debut is a masterclass in epic, cold black metal with a Viking heart, prioritizing atmosphere and medieval-style melodies over the technicality of their later work. Agnen: A Journey Through the Dark is also a core early release. |
| Glittertind | Evige Asatro | 2004 | This album blends traditional folk instruments with a punk-inflected speed, creating a "party-like" but deeply cultural homage to Norse gods and history. Til Dovre Faller is another notable EP from this era. |
| Lumsk | Åsmund Frægdegjevar | 2003 | A folk-heavy approach that uses female vocals and a wide array of traditional instruments to tell the story of a legendary Norse hero. Their later work Troll continues this exploration of Norwegian folklore and myth. |
| Skalmöld | Baldur | 2010 | Representing Iceland, this band uses triple-guitar harmonies and growled vocals to recount sagas with a focus on rhythmic intensity and authentic linguistics. Börn Loka is another essential concept album by the group. |
| Forefather | Deep into Time | 1999 | Terming their style "Anglo-Saxon Metal," this duo uses soaring melodies and programmed drums to create an epic, historical atmosphere unique to the English perspective of the era. The English Heritage is also a standout work. |
| Fyrdung | Vedergällning | 2003 | A raw, aggressive take on the genre that blends black metal vitriol with traditionalist folk melodies and lyrics focused on ancient heritage. Their album Hyperborea continues this uncompromising, primitive Viking sound. |
| Isengard | Høstmørke | 1995 | Fenriz's solo project captures the "forest-dwelling" spirit of the genre, mixing folk-rock vocals with primitive metal and doom influences. The compilation Vinterskygge is also vital for understanding the project's roots. |
| Vintersorg | Till fjälls | 1998 | This album is defined by the contrast between Andreas Hedlund's majestic baritone clean vocals and harsh screams, set against a backdrop of folk-metal melodies. Ödemarkens son is another essential record from this highly melodic era. |
| Sleipnir | Mein Anteil | 1996 | Though controversial due to political associations, this early project's musical output focused on acoustic-led ballads and mid-tempo rock that influenced the softer, "ballad" side of Viking folk-metal storytelling. |
