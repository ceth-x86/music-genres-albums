| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Skyclad | The Wayward Sons of Mother Earth | 1991 | Widely considered the first true Folk Metal album, it pioneered the fusion of thrashy riffs with Martin Walkyier's pagan-themed lyrics and prominent violin melodies. Other essential works include A Burnt Offering for the Bone Idol and Prince of the Poverty Line. |
| Cruachan | Tuatha Na Gael | 1995 | This album defined "Celtic Metal" by integrating traditional Irish tin whistles, uilleann pipes, and bodhráns into a raw black metal framework. The band further refined this folk-heavy sound on The Middle Kingdom and Folk-Lore. |
| Ensiferum | Ensiferum | 2001 | A masterclass in "Heroic Folk Metal," combining gallop-paced melodic death metal with Finnish folk melodies and choral arrangements. Their follow-up Iron is equally legendary for its epic, battle-ready atmosphere. |
| Korpiklaani | Spirit of the Forest | 2003 | Transitioning from the more acoustic Shaman, this album established the "humppa-metal" style characterized by upbeat accordion, fiddle, and lyrics centered on forest spirits and drinking. Notable later entries include Voice of Ventress and Tales Along This Road. |
| Moonsorrow | Voimasta ja kunniasta | 2001 | This record represents the "Epic Pagan Metal" ideal, featuring long, sprawling compositions that blend soaring choirs with traditional Finnish instrumentation and a cinematic atmosphere. Other masterpieces include Kivenkantaja and V: Hävitetty. |
| Primordial | The Gathering Wilderness | 2005 | Defined by a dark, mournful atmosphere, this album uses Celtic rhythmic structures and Alan Averill's passionate vocals to create a unique brand of Irish Pagan Metal. Spirit the Earth Aflame and To the Nameless Dead are also cornerstones of their discography. |
| Finntroll | Jaktens tid | 2001 | A definitive example of "Troll Metal," mixing aggressive black metal aesthetics with the bouncy rhythms of Finnish polka (humppa) and keyboard-driven folk melodies. Midnattens buskar and Nattfödd also exemplify this whimsical yet harsh style. |
| Bathory | Hammerheart | 1990 | While evolving from black metal, this album is a foundational pillar of Viking/Pagan Metal, utilizing acoustic guitars, environmental sound effects, and operatic cleans to evoke Norse mythology. Twilight of the Gods and Blood Fire Death are equally influential. |
| In Extremo | Weckt die Toten! | 1998 | This album popularized Medieval Metal by utilizing authentic period instruments like bagpipes, hurdy-gurdis, and shawms alongside heavy metal guitars. Verehrt und Angespien and Sünder ohne Zügel are also essential listening. |
| Eluveitie | Spirit | 2006 | Representing the "New Wave of Folk Metal," this record seamlessly integrates the hurdy-gurdy and flute into melodic death metal structures inspired by Gaulish history. The band's follow-up Slania further perfected this high-production Celtic sound. |
| Turisas | The Varangian Way | 2007 | A conceptual landmark that uses orchestral arrangements and accordions to narrate the journey of the Varangians, defining the "Battle Metal" sub-style. Their debut Battle Metal and the later Stand Up and Fight are also key works. |
| Månegarm | Vredens tid | 2005 | Balancing aggressive Viking metal with beautiful female vocals and prominent violin, this album captures the essence of Swedish folklore and paganism. Other notable albums include Nordstjärnans tidsålder and Dödsfärd. |
| Falkenbach | ...Magni blandinn ok megintíri... | 1998 | A quintessential one-man project album that features atmospheric synths, folk melodies, and clean/harsh vocal trade-offs dedicated to Germanic heathendom. ...En their medh ríki fara... is another vital piece of their legacy. |
| Týr | Eric the Red | 2003 | Hailing from the Faroe Islands, this band uses complex time signatures and traditional Faroese folk melodies to create a progressive form of Viking Metal. Ragnarok and The Lay of Thrym are also highly regarded for their intricate harmonies. |
| Subway to Sally | MCMXCV | 1995 | A pioneer of the German medieval scene, this album blends violin and bagpipes with a melancholic rock/metal edge. Later albums like Bannkreis and Hochzeit shifted toward a more industrial-folk sound. |
| Arkona | Ot Serdtsa K Nebu | 2007 | Leading the Slavic Pagan Metal movement, this album features Masha Scream's powerful vocals and authentic Russian folk instrumentation like the gaita and flute. Goi, Rode, Goi! and Vo Slavu Velikim! are also genre pillars. |
| Metsatöll | Hiiekoda | 2004 | This Estonian group utilizes the torupill (bagpipe) and kannel (zither) to create a gritty, mid-tempo folk metal sound rooted in local heritage. Iivakivi and Äio further explore these unique Baltic textures. |
| Equilibrium | Sagas | 2008 | An explosion of "Epic Folk Metal," featuring incredibly dense symphonic layers, high-speed tremolo picking, and jubilant folk melodies. Their debut Turis Fratyr also helped define the German scene's bombastic side. |
| Svartsot | Ravnenes Saga | 2007 | This Danish band focuses on a "low-end" folk sound, using deep growls and prominent whistles to tell tales of medieval history and folklore. Mulmets Viser is another strong example of their heavy, beer-soaked style. |
| Windir | 1184 | 2001 | Valfar's masterpiece blends "Sognametal" (a mix of black metal and folk) with electronic elements and soaring clean vocals to honor Norwegian history. Arntor and Likferd are also essential to the Pagan Metal canon. |
| Negurǎ Bunget | Om | 2006 | A transcendental take on Pagan Metal from Romania, using traditional wooden instruments and atmospheric soundscapes to evoke the spirituality of the Transylvanian mountains. Maiastru Sfetnic is another highly experimental folk-black work. |
| Heidevolk | Walhalla Wacht | 2008 | Defined by the use of dual male clean vocals, this Dutch band creates a choir-like, anthemic atmosphere focused on Germanic mythology and nature. Uit oude grond and Batavi also feature their signature harmonious sound. |
| Wolfchant | A Pagan Storm | 2007 | This album is a prime example of the German "party-folk" style, mixing melodic death metal riffs with catchy, triumphant folk lead lines. Bloody Tales of Disgraced Lands is another notable release from their early era. |
| Dalriada | Arany-album | 2009 | Hungary's premier folk metal act uses violin and dual vocals to interpret traditional Hungarian folk songs and poems within a power metal framework. Kikelet and Ígéret are also key to their discography. |
| Graveland | Thousand Swords | 1995 | A seminal work in the "Pagan Black Metal" subgenre, featuring Lo-Fi production, epic synth arrangements, and themes of ancient Slavic pride. Following the Voice of Blood is another foundational album for the movement. |
| Schandmaul | Wahre Helden | 1999 | Focusing more on the "Medieval Folk" side, this album uses bagpipes and flutes in a more melodic, less aggressive metal context that became a staple of the German scene. Von Spitzbuben und anderen Halunken is also a fan favorite. |
| Suidakra | Emprise to Decay | 2003 | This band blends melodic death metal with Celtic folk elements like acoustic guitars and banjos, often centering on historical Celtic wars. Caledonia and The Arcanum are also essential for their hybrid sound. |
| Borknagar | The Olden Domain | 1997 | An early pioneer of Progressive Pagan Metal, this album uses Garm's clean vocals and icy melodies to explore nature and philosophy. Quintessence and True North show the band's evolution into more complex territory. |
| Kampfar | Fra underverdenen | 1999 | A cold, raw representation of Norwegian Pagan Metal that avoids "cheesy" folk tropes in favor of a grim, atmospheric connection to the land and folklore. Mellom skogkledde aaser and Mare are also highly influential. |
| Nokturnal Mortum | The Goat Horns | 1997 | Combining symphonic black metal with traditional Ukrainian folk melodies and instruments, this album creates a lush, mythic atmosphere. Weltanschauung and The Voice of Steel are later masterpieces of the style. |
