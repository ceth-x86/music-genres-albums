| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Sonic Youth | Sister | 1987 | This album perfects the use of unconventional guitar tunings and physical preparation of instruments to create harmonic "halos" around melodic structures. It bridges the gap between raw noise and structured songcraft, utilizing feedback as a rhythmic and melodic tool. Other essential titles include Daydream Nation and EVOL. |
| The Birthday Party | Junkyard | 1982 | Characterized by Nick Cave's predatory vocals and Roland S. Howard's "shards of glass" guitar style, this record defines the visceral, confrontational side of art noise. The rhythm section provides a jagged, unstable foundation that emphasizes chaos over traditional groove. They also released the pivotal Prayers on Fire. |
| Swans | Public Castration Is a Good Idea | 1986 | This live document represents the absolute peak of the genre's "brutalist" phase, using punishingly slow tempos and massive walls of industrial-strength guitar noise. It transforms rock music into a transcendental, physically demanding ordeal through sheer volume and repetition. Notable studio counterparts include Filth and Cop. |
| Glenn Branca | The Ascension | 1981 | A masterwork of "guitar orchestra" composition, this album uses multiple guitars to create massive, shimmering microtonal overtones. It treats the electric guitar as a generator of pure texture and resonance rather than a solo instrument. Branca's Symphony No. 1 is another cornerstone of this approach. |
| Big Black | Songs About Fucking | 1987 | Steve Albini's use of a Roland TR-606 drum machine provides a cold, inhuman precision that contrasts with the "clanging metal" guitar tones. The production is intentionally abrasive, focusing on high-frequency treble and mechanical aggression. Atomizer is another essential document of their sound. |
| U.S. Maple | Talker | 1999 | This album represents the "deconstructionist" wing of art noise, where traditional rock rhythms are dismantled and reassembled in non-linear ways. The guitars avoid standard chords, opting for spindly, discordant lines that sound like the instruments are being pulled apart. See also Sang Phat Editor. |
| The Jesus Lizard | Goat | 1991 | Built on the foundation of David Wm. Sims' driving basslines and Duane Denison's "clean yet dissonant" guitar work, this album defines the genre's technical precision. It maintains a high-tension atmosphere that feels perpetually on the verge of a violent outburst. Liar and Head are equally definitive. |
| Unwound | Repetition | 1996 | This record blends the aggression of noise rock with sophisticated, angular song structures and a melancholic atmospheric depth. It utilizes feedback and dynamic shifts to create a "wall of sound" that remains intellectually complex. Their final opus, Leaves Turn Inside You, and New Plastic Ideas are also vital. |
| Einstürzende Neubauten | Kollaps | 1981 | By utilizing scrap metal, power tools, and industrial objects as instruments, this album redefined what constitutes "noise" in a rock context. It is a foundational text for the intersection of avant-garde art and primal sonic aggression. Drawings of Patient O.T. is another crucial release. |
| Shellac | At Action Park | 1994 | Focusing on the "physics" of sound, this album features ultra-dry production and a focus on the percussive qualities of the electric guitar. The interplay between the instruments is mathematical and sparse, leaving massive gaps for silence and sudden noise bursts. 1000 Hurts is another landmark title. |
| The Ex | Starters Alternators | 1998 | Combining frantic, polyrhythmic drumming with sharp, "scratchy" guitar textures, this album showcases the genre's rhythmic complexity. It draws from both punk energy and avant-garde jazz structures to create a unique, agitated sound. Turn and A Fistful of Spanners are also essential. |
| Daughters | You Won't Get What You Want | 2018 | A modern masterpiece of art noise, this record uses guitars to mimic industrial synthesizers and sirens, creating an oppressive, claustrophobic atmosphere. The vocals are delivered as a frantic, desperate spoken-word performance over a chaotic sonic backdrop. Their self-titled 2010 album is also significant. |
| Melt-Banana | Fetch | 2013 | Representing the hyper-kinetic "Cyberpunk" side of noise rock, this album features lightning-fast tempos and glitch-like guitar effects. It treats noise as a colorful, high-energy palette rather than a dark or brooding one. Cell-Scape and Charlie are other definitive works. |
| Lightning Bolt | Wonderful Rainbow | 2003 | This duo-led assault relies on distorted, high-register bass guitar and manic drumming to create a whirlwind of sound. It captures the "art-noise" spirit through its sheer rejection of standard guitar melody in favor of frequency-shifting chaos. Hypermagic Mountain is another mandatory listen. |
| Liars | Drum's Not Dead | 2006 | This conceptual work focuses on tribal, polyrhythmic percussion and eerie, textured noise instead of traditional riffs. It emphasizes the "Art" in Art Noise by creating a haunting, experimental narrative through sound. They Were Wrong, So We Drowned is another key experimental title. |
| DNA | A Taste of DNA | 1981 | A core "No Wave" document, this album features Arto Lindsay's completely non-tonal guitar playing and fractured rhythmic structures. It is the archetype for minimalist noise that prioritizes texture and "wrongness" over musicality. Their contributions to the No New York compilation are also essential. |
| Pussy Galore | Right Now! | 1987 | Defined by a "trash-aesthetic," this album uses multiple distorted guitars and a metal-drum kit to create a chaotic, lo-fi wall of sound. It is a deconstruction of blues-rock through the lens of pure noise and nihilism. Dial M for Motherfucker is another notable release. |
| Chat Pile | God's Country | 2022 | This contemporary release utilizes sludge-heavy riffs and a disturbing, narrative-driven vocal style to depict urban decay. The "noise" here comes from the oppressive, industrial-like weight of the instrumentation and the shrieking feedback interludes. Their Remove Your Skin Please EP is also vital. |
| Boredoms | Pop Tatari | 1992 | This album is an explosion of "maximalist" noise, blending psych-rock, punk, and avant-garde electronics into a bewildering collage. It defines the more playful and chaotic side of the genre's Japanese lineage. Vision Creation Newsun shows their later, more rhythmic evolution. |
| Mars | 78 | 1978 | One of the most abstract bands of the original New York scene, Mars used guitars as percussive, dissonant objects. This compilation of their early work showcases a total abandonment of rock melody in favor of surreal, jagged noise. Their tracks on No New York are foundational. |
| Gilla Band | Holding Hands with Jamie | 2015 | This record uses extreme pedal effects to make guitars sound like malfunctioning techno synthesizers or industrial drills. The repetitive, motorik rhythms create a hypnotic yet deeply unsettling "art-noise" experience. The Talkies further pushed these experimental boundaries. |
| Health | Health | 2007 | Their debut is a masterclass in using "noise-as-percussion," featuring heavy use of feedback, tribal drumming, and processed vocal chants. It is distinct for its focus on rhythmic density and short, sharp sonic bursts. Get Color is another essential evolution of this style. |
| Oxbow | The Narcotic Story | 2007 | Mixing avant-garde jazz structures with explosive noise-rock, this album is defined by Eugene Robinson's idiosyncratic, theatrical vocal delivery. The music shifts unpredictably between eerie quiet and orchestral-scale noise. An Evil Heat is another definitive chapter. |
| Arab on Radar | Yahweh or the Highway | 2001 | Characterized by high-pitched, "skronky" guitars and panicked, shrieking vocals, this album defines the abrasive "Squealing" sub-set of noise rock. The rhythms are jittery and unsettling, avoiding any sense of comfort. Rougher It is also highly recommended. |
| Theoretical Girls | Theoretical Record | 1978 | Featuring Glenn Branca and Rhys Chatham, this band applied classical minimalist theories to loud, distorted rock music. The result is a proto-noise rock sound that is both intellectually rigid and sonically violent. Their singles are collected on various retrospective compilations. |
| Les Rallizes Dénudés | '77 Live | 1991 | This Japanese ensemble defined the "psychedelic noise" direction, using excessive levels of feedback to completely bury their melodic structures. It is a study in sonic endurance and the beauty of overdriven amplification. Blind Baby Has Its Mother's Eyes is another key bootleg/release. |
| Butthole Surfers | Locust Abortion Technician | 1987 | This album uses tape manipulation, pitch-shifted vocals, and heavy sludge-noise to create a "nightmare" version of psychedelic rock. It represents the genre's most surreal and experimental tendencies. Psychic... Powerless... Another Man's Sac is also essential. |
| Blacklisters | Bltant | 2012 | A prime example of modern noise rock that focuses on "groove-based" dissonance and sarcastic, aggressive vocal delivery. The production is massive, emphasizing the percussive impact of the bass and drums. Adult is their other major contribution to the genre. |
| A Place to Bury Strangers | Exploding Head | 2009 | While leaning toward shoegaze, this album is defined by its "wall of death" volume and custom-built effects pedals that generate pure sonic chaos. It focuses on the physical sensation of noise as an artistic medium. Their Self-Titled debut is equally punishing. |
| Whores. | Gold. | 2016 | This album represents the "heavy" end of the spectrum, utilizing massive, distorted riffs that emphasize the "rock" in noise rock without losing the artful aggression. The focus is on crushing tonal density and cathartic, yelled vocals. Their EPs Ruiner and Clean are foundational. |
