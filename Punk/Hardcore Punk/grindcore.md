# Grindcore

Grindcore is an extreme music genre that emerged in the mid-1980s from the collision of hardcore punk's velocity and aggression with death metal's heaviness and brutality. Characterized by blast beats, microsecond song lengths, guttural or shrieked vocals, and heavily distorted guitars tuned to crushing low frequencies, the genre strips music down to its most visceral and confrontational essence. Originating primarily in the UK with bands on the Earache Records label and simultaneously in the American underground, grindcore emphasizes raw, lo-fi production aesthetics and a DIY punk ethos. Lyrically, the genre splits between politically charged socio-economic critique and the graphic medical and gore-obsessed themes of the goregrind subgenre. Grindcore distinguishes itself from death metal through its punk-derived brevity and structural simplicity, and from hardcore punk through its extreme tempo, inhuman vocal techniques, and metal-influenced guitar tones, occupying a uniquely uncompromising space at the intersection of both traditions.
