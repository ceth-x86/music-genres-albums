# Cloud Rap

Cloud rap is a subgenre of hip-hop that emerged in the late 2000s and early 2010s, characterized by its hazy, atmospheric production built on reverb-drenched synthesizers, ethereal vocal samples, and a dreamy, dissociative sonic aesthetic. Pioneered by producers like Clams Casino and artists like Lil B and Main Attrakionz, the genre takes its name from its "floating" quality — beats that feel spacious, washed-out, and otherworldly compared to the hard-hitting drums of mainstream rap. The sound is defined by heavy use of reverb and delay on both vocals and instrumentals, lo-fi production techniques, auto-tuned or pitched vocal deliveries, and a lyrical approach that emphasizes mood, stream-of-consciousness flow, and emotional vulnerability over technical precision. Cloud rap distinguishes itself from trap through its atmospheric spaciousness and slower, more contemplative energy, and from emo rap through its origins in internet-based DIY culture and sample-based beatmaking rather than pop-punk influences. The genre developed strong international branches, particularly the Swedish "sad boys" movement, and influenced the broader evolution of SoundCloud-era hip-hop.
