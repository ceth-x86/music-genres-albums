# Jazz Rap

Jazz rap is a fusion genre that emerged in the late 1980s and flourished through the 1990s, combining hip-hop's rhythmic vocal delivery and boom-bap production with jazz's harmonic sophistication, improvisational spirit, and instrumental palette of upright bass, piano, horns, and brushed drums. The sound is characterized by sample-based production that draws heavily from jazz records — particularly hard bop, modal jazz, and jazz fusion — creating warm, melodic backdrops of looping horn phrases, walking basslines, and filtered chord progressions over which MCs deliver laid-back, often intellectual and introspective lyrics. Pioneered by the Native Tongues collective, A Tribe Called Quest, Gang Starr, and Guru's Jazzmatazz project, jazz rap represented hip-hop's bohemian, Afrocentric wing that valued musical sophistication and cultural consciousness over street narratives. The genre distinguishes itself from standard boom-bap through its explicit jazz sourcing and emphasis on melodic warmth, and from jazz fusion through its retention of hip-hop's vocal centrality and sample-based construction rather than live improvisation. Jazz rap's influence extends through lo-fi hip-hop, neo-soul, and the continued tradition of crate-digging producers who mine jazz catalogs for their rhythmic and harmonic richness.
