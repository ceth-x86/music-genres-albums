# Mumble Rap

Mumble Rap is a loosely defined but widely recognized strain of hip-hop that emerged in the mid-2010s, characterized by its prioritization of melodic flow, vocal texture, and atmospheric "vibe" over lyrical clarity and traditional rapping technique. The style is rooted in Southern trap production—heavy 808 bass, skittering hi-hats, and dark synthesizer melodies—but distinguishes itself through its vocal approach: slurred, Auto-Tuned, and often intentionally indistinct delivery where the voice functions more as a melodic instrument than a vehicle for narrative. Pioneered by artists like Future and Young Thug and popularized through the SoundCloud platform by a wave of younger artists, the genre embraces repetition, ad-libs, and minimalist lyrical structures that emphasize catchy hooks and rhythmic cadences. Production tends toward atmospheric, beat-driven soundscapes where the rapper's voice blends into the instrumental rather than sitting atop it. The style drew fierce debate within hip-hop culture over the value of lyricism versus sonic innovation, but its commercial dominance through the late 2010s reshaped the genre's mainstream sound. It directly influenced and overlapped with adjacent movements including emo-rap, SoundCloud rap, and melodic trap.

| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Future | DS2 | 2015 | This album is the gold standard for the genre, featuring "slurred" vocal delivery over heavy, drug-addled trap production by Metro Boomin. It emphasizes atmosphere and melodic flows over lyrical clarity, as also heard on his albums Monster and 56 Nights. |
| Young Thug | Barter 6 | 2015 | Thugger's unique vocal gymnastics, characterized by squeaks, murmurs, and unconventional syntax, pushed the boundaries of how a voice can be used as an instrument. His projects Jeffery and So Much Fun further explore this fluid, avant-garde approach to melodic rap. |
| Migos | Culture | 2017 | Known for popularizing the "triplet flow," this album uses repetitive, rhythmic cadences and heavy ad-libs to create a catchy, hypnotic energy. The group's earlier mixtape Young Rich Niggas was equally instrumental in establishing this staccato mumble style. |
| Playboi Carti | Die Lit | 2018 | This album stripped rap down to its most primal elements, focusing on "baby voice" vocals and repetitive mantras over bright, experimental Pi'erre Bourne beats. Carti's self-titled debut and Whole Lotta Red also define this minimalism-meets-punk energy. |
| Lil Uzi Vert | Luv Is Rage 2 | 2017 | Uzi blends emo-inflected melodies with rapid-fire, semi-coherent delivery that prioritizes emotional "vibe" over storytelling. Essential follow-ups like Eternal Atake and Lil Uzi Vert vs. The World solidified their status as a genre-bending icon. |
| Travis Scott | Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight | 2016 | While highly produced, this album epitomizes the "auto-tuned croon" and dark, atmospheric trap textures that define the genre's aesthetic. His massive hit Astroworld and the dark Rodeo are also foundational to this psychedelic sound. |
| 21 Savage | Issa Album | 2017 | Featuring a distinctively monotone, whispered delivery, this album brings a "dead-eyed" realism to the genre's typical bravado. His collaborations with Metro Boomin, such as Savage Mode and Savage Mode II, are equally quintessential. |
| Kodak Black | Painting Pictures | 2017 | Kodak's thick Floridian drawl and slurred enunciation create a raw, unpolished sound that feels both soulful and incoherent. Notable other works include Project Baby 2 and Dying to Live, which maintain this gritty, melodic style. |
| Lil Yachty | Lil Boat | 2016 | This mixtape pioneered "bubblegum trap," using off-key singing and playful, mumbly delivery over whimsical, video-game-inspired beats. His later work like Teenage Emotions and the psychedelic Let's Start Here show his evolution from these roots. |
| Chief Keef | Finally Rich | 2012 | While technically Drill, Keef's slurred delivery and emphasis on "vibes" over lyricism laid the architectural groundwork for the entire mumble rap movement. Mixtapes like Bang 2 and Almighty So pushed his vocal experimentation even further into abstraction. |
| Desiigner | New English | 2016 | Primarily known for the hit "Panda," Desiigner's style is characterized by high-energy vocalizations, heavy use of ad-libs, and a delivery heavily influenced by Future's gravelly slur. His L.O.D. EP continues this high-octane, percussive vocal style. |
| Rich The Kid | The World Is Yours | 2018 | This album features the quintessential "plug" sound, with rapid, often unintelligible flows over bouncy, melodic trap production. His mixtape series Rich Forever also highlights his ability to weave through beats with loose, rhythmic mumbles. |
| Lil Pump | Lil Pump | 2017 | Defined by extreme repetition and distorted, lo-fi production, this album represents the genre at its most simplified and high-energy. His follow-up Harverd Dropout maintains the same formula of catchy, shouted hooks and minimal lyrical depth. |
| Gunna | Drip or Drown 2 | 2019 | Gunna is a master of the "effortless flow," where his voice blends seamlessly into the guitar-heavy trap beats, often sacrificing clarity for a smooth, liquid-like melody. His Drip Season series is equally vital to the "drip" sub-aesthetic. |
| Lil Baby | Harder Than Ever | 2018 | Baby utilizes a fast-paced, high-pitched mumble that creates a sense of urgency and constant motion over rhythmic bass lines. My Turn and his collaborative album Voice of the Heroes further perfected this dominant commercial sound. |
| Rae Sremmurd | SremmLife | 2015 | This duo brought a high-pitched, party-centric energy to the genre, emphasizing catchy phrases and melodic shouting over intricate lyricism. SremmLife 2 and the triple-album SR3MM continued their streak of melodic, mumbly club anthems. |
| Famous Dex | Dex Meets Dexter | 2018 | Dex uses a chaotic, high-energy delivery filled with quirky ad-libs and repetitive hooks that define the "SoundCloud" era of the genre. His mixtapes Rich Forever and Dexter the Robot are also prime examples of this manic style. |
| Nav | NAV | 2017 | Known for a heavily auto-tuned, robotic delivery that stays within a narrow emotional range, Nav emphasizes the "drone" aspect of the genre. His albums Bad Habits and Good Intentions refined this clean, XO-inspired trap sound. |
| A Boogie wit da Hoodie | The Bigger Artist | 2017 | This album bridges the gap between traditional New York rap and the melodic, slurred delivery of the South, focusing on catchy, sing-song hooks. Hoodie SZN and Artist 2.0 further cement his melodic, mumbly approach. |
| Juice WRLD | Goodbye & Good Riddance | 2018 | While leaning into Emo-Rap, Juice WRLD's freestyle-based delivery often involved slurred, melodic ruminations that fit the core mumble rap profile. His album Death Race for Love and the posthumous Legends Never Die are also essential. |
| Trippie Redd | Life's a Trip | 2018 | Trippie combines melodic mumbles with rock-inspired wails, creating a highly emotive and textured vocal style over versatile trap beats. His A Love Letter to You series is a cornerstone of this melodic, "mumble-adjacent" movement. |
| 6ix9ine | Day69 | 2018 | Though known for shouting, 6ix9ine's style relies on repetitive, aggressive cadences and minimal lyrical complexity that mirrors the mumble rap structure. His album Dummy Boy followed the same commercially successful, high-decibel formula. |
| BlocBoy JB | Simi | 2018 | This album showcases the "Memphis" influence on the genre, featuring percussive, stuttering flows and heavy emphasis on viral dance-ready hooks. His project Fat Boy continues this trend of simple, rhythmic, and high-energy rap. |
| Lil Skies | Life of a Dark Rose | 2018 | Skies utilizes a smooth, melodic delivery that often prioritizes the "feel" of the track over distinct word pronunciation. His follow-up Shelby further explored this accessible, radio-friendly version of the mumble sound. |
| Smokepurpp | Deadstar | 2017 | A central figure in the SoundCloud scene, Smokepurpp uses distorted beats and a hazy, drug-influenced delivery to create a dark, aggressive atmosphere. Florida Jit and Lost Planet also reflect this gritty, lo-fi aesthetic. |
| Ski Mask The Slump God | You Will Regret | 2017 | While technically a very skilled lyricist, Ski Mask's rapid-fire, "cartoonish" delivery and eccentric ad-libs often border on the abstract "mumble" territory. His album Stokeley showcases his ability to blend speed with incoherent, playful melodies. |
| Blueface | Famous Cryp | 2018 | Famous for his "off-beat" flow, Blueface's delivery is a literal mumble-slur that often ignores the tempo of the beat entirely for a unique rhythmic effect. The Dirt Bag EP features more of this divisive, high-pitched West Coast style. |
| Roddy Ricch | Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial | 2019 | Roddy uses a highly melodic, fluid delivery that often sees his voice cracking and shifting in pitch, creating a "liquid" mumble effect. His Feed Tha Streets series is also vital for understanding his rise in the melodic trap scene. |
| Don Toliver | Heaven or Hell | 2020 | Toliver's style is defined by a unique, nasal croon that prioritizes texture and "vibe" over lyrical clarity, often acting as a melodic layer to the production. His work on Life of a DON and Love Sick further explores this psychedelic, slurred sound. |
| Yeat | Up 2 Më | 2021 | Representing the newest wave, Yeat uses high-pitched, bells-infused production and a heavily slurred, inventive slang-filled delivery. His albums 2 Alivë and Afterlyfe have pushed the genre into a more futuristic, digital territory. |
