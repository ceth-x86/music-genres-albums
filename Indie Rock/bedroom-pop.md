| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Clairo | Diary 001 | 2018 | This EP is the commercial "big bang" of the genre, featuring hazy, lo-fi synths and Casio-style drum patterns recorded in a private domestic space. Her deadpan, intimate vocal delivery on tracks like "Hello?" and "4EVER" established the template for the genre's relatability; her full-length Immunity is also a cornerstone of this sound. |
| Cuco | Wannabewithu | 2016 | Melding Chicano soul influences with "dreamy" lo-fi production, this album uses warped synthesizers and romantic, bilingual lyrics to create a distinctively nostalgic atmosphere. It captures the DIY essence of a young artist producing hits from a home computer; his follow-up Songs4u further refined this "dream-hop" aesthetic. |
| Rex Orange County | Apricot Princess | 2017 | This record defines the genre's "awkward-earnest" lyrical spirit, blending bright, jazzy keyboard chords with unpolished, heartfelt vocals about teenage longing. While more musically ambitious than some lo-fi peers, its "home-cooked" charm remains central; his debut Bcc is another essential example of his early DIY roots. |
| Panchiko | Deathmetal | 2000 | A cult masterpiece of the "lost media" era, this album features distorted, bit-crushed electronic textures and melancholic vocals that sound like they were recovered from a degraded disc. Its accidental "rot" aesthetic became a massive influence on modern lo-fi producers; the 2020 compilation of their early works is also highly regarded. |
| Beabadoobee | Patched Up | 2018 | Centered around delicate acoustic guitar and whisper-quiet vocals, this release epitomizes the "confessional" side of the genre before it moved toward 90s alt-rock. It feels like a private diary entry set to music, utilizing minimal production to highlight raw emotion; Loveworm is another key release in her early lo-fi catalog. |
| Boy Pablo | Roy Pablo | 2017 | Built on jangly, chorus-heavy guitars and upbeat but self-deprecating lyrics, this EP defines the "sunny-yet-sad" paradox of indie-adjacent bedroom pop. The production is clean but retains a clear "garage-to-bedroom" feel that helped launch the genre into the mainstream; Soy Pablo is equally definitive for its catchy, surf-pop influence. |
| The Maria's | Superclean, Vol. 1 | 2017 | This EP blends smooth lounge jazz with bedroom-produced synth-pop, creating a velvet-textured, "cinematic" lo-fi sound. The hushed, breathy vocals and retro-futuristic production embody the "aesthetic" side of the genre; Superclean, Vol. 2 continues this polished yet intimate trajectory. |
| Gus Dapperton | Yellow and Such | 2017 | Featuring eccentric vocal inflections and colorful, quirky synth arrangements, this record showcases the more experimental and visual-heavy side of the DIY movement. The tracks feel like curated art pieces recorded in a high-fashion basement; You Think You're a Comic! is another vital entry in his discography. |
| Girl in Red | chapter 1 | 2018 | This EP is a quintessential example of "bedroom rock," utilizing simple guitar riffs and extremely direct, queer-coded lyrics to build a massive online community. The production is fuzzy and uncompressed, prioritizing the urgency of the message over studio perfection; chapter 2 serves as its perfect companion piece. |
| Joji | In Tongues | 2017 | Transitioning from internet comedy to serious artistry, Joji utilized dark, trip-hop-influenced beats and muffled, soul-searching vocals to define "sad-boy" bedroom pop. The heavy use of foley sounds and piano loops creates a claustrophobic, late-night mood; his debut LP Ballads 1 expanded this sound into more melodic territory. |
| Sales | Sales EP | 2014 | Minimalist to its core, this album uses little more than a rhythmic guitar pluck and vocal harmonies to create catchy, skeletal pop songs. Its "less-is-more" approach proved that bedroom pop doesn't need layers of synths to be effective; their self-titled 2016 album Sales LP is also a genre staple. |
| Temporex | Care | 2017 | This album is a masterclass in "wonky" lo-fi, featuring pitch-shifted vocals and wobbly, warbling synthesizers that sound like a warped cassette tape. It captures the playful, childlike wonder often found in the genre's early Soundcloud era; his subsequent singles continue this "off-kilter" electronic pop style. |
| Ricky Montgomery | Montgomery Ricky | 2016 | Bringing a more structured, singer-songwriter approach to the DIY scene, this album features bright melodies and lyrics focused on domesticity and young adulthood. The production balances the clarity of pop with the grit of independent recording; his earlier singles like "Line Without a Hook" remain genre anthems. |
| Bane's World | Drowsy | 2016 | Embodying the "slacker" side of the genre, this record is drenched in reverb and slow-tempo, 60s-inspired soul melodies. The atmosphere is hazy and lethargic, perfectly capturing the feeling of a sun-drenched afternoon spent indoors; his collaborations with other lo-fi artists have further cemented his influence. |
| Jakob Ogawa | Bedroom Tapes | 2017 | With its heavy 80s synth influence and high-pitched, smooth vocals, this EP explores the "sophisti-pop" side of bedroom production. It feels both glamorous and lonely, utilizing digital textures to create a lush, private world; his single "All Your Love" is another essential track in this style. |
| Conan Gray | Sunset Season | 2018 | Before becoming a stadium pop star, Gray recorded these tracks in his bedroom, focusing on the trials of suburban teenage life. The EP uses programmed drums and "found sound" textures to bridge the gap between DIY indie and mainstream pop; his YouTube-born singles like "Idle Town" are the genre's literal origin stories. |
| Inner Wave | Underwater Pipe Dreams | 2017 | This album leans into the psychedelic and "groovier" side of the genre, mixing Latin-inspired rhythms with lo-fi garage rock. The muffled production and synth-heavy bridges create a "sun-baked" psychedelic experience recorded in a home studio; their earlier work Sun Transmission is also highly recommended. |
| Kavinsky | Teddy Boy | 2006 | While often associated with Synthwave, this early release pioneered the "bedroom electronic" aesthetic of using cheap gear to create nostalgic, filmic pop. Its influence on the "aesthetic" and "retro-future" visual side of bedroom pop cannot be overstated; the album OutRun is his more polished, mainstream follow-up. |
| Still Woozy | Woozy Confession | 2019 | This collection of singles defines the "psychedelic-funk" sub-branch of the genre, characterized by bouncy basslines and highly processed, layered vocal harmonies. The tracks feel modern and "glitchy" yet remain grounded in a warm, domestic intimacy; his debut album If This Isn't Nice, I Don't Know What Is expands on this sound. |
| Current Joys | Wild Heart | 2013 | A cornerstone of the "lo-fi surf/goth" sound, this album features driving drum machines and simple, repetitive guitar lines paired with evocative, emotive vocals. It captures the "post-punk" energy that often informs the darker corners of bedroom pop; his project Surf Curse also explores similar DIY territory. |
| Cosmo Pyke | Just For This | 2017 | Blending jazz, reggae, and indie-pop, this EP represents the UK "South London" bedroom scene with its relaxed, improvisational feel. The production is crisp but maintains a "live-in-a-room" quality that highlights the artist's instrumental skill; his single "Chronic Sunshine" is a definitive genre anthem. |
| Yellow Days | Harmless Melodies | 2016 | Defined by a deep, gravelly vocal performance and "drunken" guitar chords, this record brings a bluesy soul sensibility to the bedroom pop format. The heavy use of chorus pedals and slow tempos creates a melancholic, immersive world; Is Everything Okay in Your World? is another essential, sprawling work. |
| Her's | Invitation to Her's | 2018 | This duo perfected the "80s-inspired jangle" with drum machines that sound like vintage presets and surf-rock guitar leads. Their sound is theatrical yet intimate, blending humor with genuine longing in a way that is central to the genre's identity; Songs of Her's is a vital compilation of their early hits. |
| Mxmtoon | plum blossom | 2018 | Built around the ukulele—a staple instrument of the genre's early YouTube era—this EP focuses on vulnerability and "internet-age" anxiety. The production is sparse, letting the close-mic'd vocals create a sense of sitting right next to the listener; her debut LP the masquerade expanded this into a fuller pop sound. |
| Peachy! | Under the Sun | 2018 | This album exemplifies the intersection of "Lo-Fi Hip Hop" beats and bedroom pop vocals, featuring relaxed rhythms and warm acoustic samples. The atmosphere is purely "chill," designed for background listening or quiet reflection; the hit single "Falling for U" (with Mxmtoon) is a genre-defining collaboration. |
| Mild High Club | Timeline | 2015 | This record utilizes 70s AOR and "yacht rock" influences, filtered through a hazy, lo-fi lens to create "stoner-pop" for the bedroom. The keyboard-driven arrangements are complex but the recording quality remains delightfully "muddy" and nostalgic; Skiptracing is their most popular and definitive work. |
| Fog Lake | Dragonfly | 2017 | Representing the "slowcore" and ambient side of the genre, this album features washed-out guitars and ghostly, distant vocals. It captures the feeling of isolation and late-night insomnia better than almost any other record in the scene; Captain and Inferno are also essential for fans of dark lo-fi. |
| Homeshake | Midnight Snack | 2015 | Characterized by "sleepy" synthesizers and skeletal R&B grooves, this album defined the "slacker-soul" aesthetic that many bedroom producers mimic. The high-pitched, nonchalant vocals and dry drum sounds create a unique sense of domestic lethargy; In the Shower is another key text in the Homeshake catalog. |
| Mac DeMarco | Salad Days | 2014 | While he eventually moved to professional studios, this album is the "spiritual godfather" of the bedroom pop sound, popularizing the "detuned" guitar and DIY ethos. The jangly, warbling melodies and laid-back attitude provided the blueprint for an entire generation of home recorders; 2 is equally essential for its raw, indie-rock simplicity. |
| Cavetown | Lemon Boy | 2018 | Mixing folk-pop sensibilities with electronic flourishes, this album deals with themes of mental health and identity in a deeply personal, DIY manner. The production feels like a "digital scrapbook," combining acoustic instruments with bedroom-synth textures; his self-titled 2015 debut is also a foundational lo-fi work. |
