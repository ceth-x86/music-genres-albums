| Artist | Album | Year | Why it defines the genre |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Heilung | Ofnir | 2015 | This album uses "amplified history," featuring bone percussion, running water, and throat singing to recreate Iron Age Norse rituals. Other essential works include Futha and Drif. |
| Wardruna | Runaljod - Gap Var Ginnunga | 2009 | It utilizes historical instruments like the kravik lyre and goat horns alongside natural sounds to interpret the Elder Futhark runes. Notable later chapters include Yggdrasil and Ragnarok. |
| Danheim | Munarvagr | 2017 | Known for deep, rhythmic chanting and dark atmospheric layers, it creates a cinematic yet primal Viking-era ritual experience. The artist also explored these themes in Heritages and Skapanir. |
| Forndom | Dauðra Dura | 2016 | This project focuses on the somber, melancholic side of Old Norse spirituality, using slow-build atmospheres and minimalist percussion. The albums Flykt and Faþir are equally vital to the genre. |
| Sowulo | SOL | 2016 | Focusing on the cycles of the sun, this album blends acoustic strings and group vocals to evoke a communal, celebratory pagan ritual. Wurtar and Ginnungagap further refine this archetypal sound. |
| The Moon and the Nightspirit | Mohaliv | 2011 | This Hungarian duo blends tribal rhythms with traditional woodwinds to create a shamanic, forest-dwelling atmosphere. Regő Rejtem and Metanoia are also hallmarks of their ritualistic style. |
| Nytt Land | Oðal | 2018 | Utilizing Siberian throat singing and hand-crafted instruments, this album bridges the gap between Norse myth and Mongolian steppe ritual. Other significant releases include Torem and Ritual. |
| Tenhi | Kauan | 1999 | A foundational Finnish work that uses folk instrumentation and dark, repetitive structures to evoke the quiet, ritualistic nature of the Northern wilderness. Märe and Väre are also highly recommended. |
| Nebala | Lust | 2021 | Led by Jonas Lorentzen (ex-Heilung), this album focuses on the visceral, erotic, and ecstatic side of ancient rituals through polyphonic singing. It stands as a modern cornerstone alongside the Hymns of the Night EP. |
| Gealdýr | Sól | 2019 | This album emphasizes vocal melodies and atmospheric chants intended to guide the listener into a meditative, spiritual state. Oðinn and Valhalla explore similar mythological depths. |
| Daemonia Nymphe | Psychostasia | 2005 | Using replicas of ancient Greek instruments, they recreate the Orphic and Dionysian mystery rites through ethereal vocals and lyre-driven arrangements. The Bacchic Dance of the Nymphs is another core title. |
| Osi and the Jupiter | Halls of the Slain | 2017 | Centered on the cello and deep drones, this project evokes the solitary, spiritual journey through the Appalachian and Northern forests. Uthark Runatyr and Stave are essential companion pieces. |
| Eihwar | Viking War Trance | 2024 | A modern evolution of the genre that combines heavy acoustic tribal percussion with repetitive, trance-inducing chants for a modern ritual setting. They are also known for their Ragnarök performances. |
| Draugurinn | Í dvala | 2017 | This project leans into the "dark" side of ritual folk, using deep, cavernous drones and minimal bone-clash percussion to evoke funeral rites. Ósköp and Minni are equally haunting works. |
| Skáld | Le Chant des Vikings | 2019 | Focusing on skaldic poetry, this album uses the talharpa and percussive chants to tell tales of the Eddas in a rhythmic, ritualistic fashion. Winter's Tales and Huldufólk are notable follow-ups. |
| Kati Rán | SÁLYA | 2024 | Blending Nordic folk with cinematic production, she uses the nyckelharpa and water-based sounds to explore the feminine divine in ritual. Her previous work Völuspá is a genre staple. |
| Sangre de Muerdago | Xuntas | 2020 | While leaning toward forest folk, the complex polyphonic vocals and hypnotic nylon strings create a profound sense of Galician ritualism. Noite and O Camiño das Estrelas are also vital. |
| Nest | Woodsmoke | 2003 | Built around the kantele, this album creates a quiet, secluded ritual atmosphere that feels deeply tied to Finnish forest folklore. Trail of the Green-Eyed Wolf is another significant entry. |
| Munknörr | Fimbulwinter | 2019 | This album uses lush, atmospheric textures and steady drum beats to create a backdrop for shamanic journeying and meditation. Eitun and Vinland offer similar immersive experiences. |
| Runa | The Ritual | 2018 | Focusing on the rhythmic pulse of the earth, this album uses frame drums and low-register chants to ground the listener in a pagan reality. Yggdrasil (not the Wardruna album) is a notable related work. |
| Valkyrie Seidr | Solar Hymns | 2020 | A project dedicated to the solar cycle, featuring soaring female vocals and repetitive, hypnotic drum patterns that mimic ancient communal rites. Moon Rituals serves as its lunar counterpart. |
| Kjell Braaten | Ferd | 2020 | A multi-instrumentalist who contributed to Wardruna, his solo work focuses on the technical and spiritual purity of ancient Scandinavian sounds. Blót is another essential ritual recording. |
| Hindarfjäll | Liktida | 2021 | This album explores the transition between life and death through somber vocal harmonies and traditional Swedish folk instruments. Från tidernas begynnelse is also a key release. |
| Aethelwyne | The High Heat of Summer | 2019 | Utilizing Anglo-Saxon themes, this work blends field recordings with acoustic arrangements to celebrate the seasonal shifts of the natural world. Winter Solstice is another defining piece. |
| Arstidir Lifsins | Saga á tveim tungum I | 2019 | While incorporating elements of metal, their purely acoustic passages are masterclasses in atmospheric, historical ritual storytelling. Saga á tveim tungum II completes the cycle. |
| Garmarna | Förbundet | 2020 | Though often considered "folk rock," this specific album strips back the electronics to reveal a dark, ritualistic heart based on medieval Swedish ballads. Vedergällningen is their other essential milestone. |
| Ivar Bjørnson & Einar Selvik | Hugsjá | 2018 | A collaboration exploring the history of Norway through grand, percussive compositions and traditional Norse instruments. Their first collaboration, Skuggsjá, is equally definitive. |
| Ulvesang | The Lepidopterist | 2018 | Minimalist neo-folk that utilizes repetitive acoustic guitar patterns to create a trance-like, ritualistic state of isolation. Their self-titled debut, Ulvesang, set the tone for this sound. |
| Kalandra | The Line | 2020 | Blending ethereal pop with deep Nordic roots, their use of traditional vocal techniques like 'kulning' provides a haunting, ritualistic atmosphere. Their soundtrack for Kingdom Two Crowns is also noteworthy. |
| Kaunan | Forn | 2017 | Featuring members from Wardruna and Forndom, this trio focuses on the raw, dance-like energy of ancient Northern European folk music. It serves as a more upbeat, communal form of ritual. |
